WWE UK-Only PPV Results

WWF/E UK-ONLY PPV RESULTS

27th September 1997: “WWF One Night Only”: Birmingham, England – 11,000
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Hunter Hearst Helmsley beat Dude Love; Tiger Ali Singh downed Leif Cassidy; WWF World Tag Champions The Headbangers dropped Savio Vega and Miguel Perez; The Patriot dropped Flash Funk; The Legion of Doom overcame The Godwinns; Vader toppled Owen hart; WWF World Champion Bret Hart bested The Undertaker by DQ; Shawn Michaels rolled over WWF European Champion Davey Boy Smith by stoppage to win the title.

6th December 1998: “WWF Capital Carnage”:  London, England – 10,441
($412,147 live gate)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Gangrel downed Al Snow; The Headbangers defeated Road Warrior Animal and Droz; Val Venis pinned Goldust; Tiger Ali Singh dropped Edge; Christian and WWF Women’s Champion Sable bested Marc Mero and Jackie; WWF Intercontinental Champion Ken Shamrock toppled Steve Blackman; Triple H bested Jeff Jarrett; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg overturned Mark Henry and D-Lo Brown; WWF World Champion The Rock overcame WWF European Champion X-Pac by DQ; Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker, Kane and Mankind in a Fatal Four Way. Gerald Briscoe was guest referee and The Big Bossman was guest ringside enforcer.

16th May 1999: “WWF No Mercy 1999 (UK)”: Manchester, England – 18,107 sellout
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Tiger Ali Singh beat WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Gillberg in a non-title match; Viscera and The Acolytes defeated Edge, Christian and Gangrel; Steve Blackman downed Droz; Kane dropped Mideon by DQ; Nicole Bass overcame Tori; WWF European Champion Shane McMahon overturned X-Pac; Billy Gunn toppled Mankind; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker and Triple H in a “No-Holds-Barred” Triple Threat match.

2nd October 1999: “WWF Rebellion 1999”: Birmingham, England – 11,939 sellout
($467,494 live gate)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
WWF Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett bested WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown; The Godfather defeated Gangrel; Val Venis overcame Mark Henry; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory downed Tori, Luna Vachon and Jackie in a Fatal Four Way; Chris Jericho toppled Road Dogg; Chyna overturned WWF Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett by DQ; Kane pinned The Big Show in a no-DQ match; Davey Boy Smith dropped X-Pac; Edge and Christian overcame The Acolytes, and Hardcore and Crash Holly in a Triple Threat elimination match; WWF World Champion Triple H rolled over The Rock in a cage match.

6th May 2000: “WWF Insurrextion 2000”: London, England – 17,000
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Too Cool beat Perry Saturn and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko; Kane downed Bull Buchanan; Road Dogg dropped Bradshaw; The Kat overcame Terri in an arm-wrestling match; Rikishi and The Big Show toppled The Dudley Boys; Kurt Angle pinned Chris Benoit; Davey Boy Smith pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Matt and Jeff Hardy defeated WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian by DQ; WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero bested WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over Shane McMahon and Triple H in a Triple Threat match.

2nd December 2000: “WWF Rebellion 2000”: Sheffield, England – 11,077 sellout
($536,866 live gate) (GBP£378,075)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
PPV Broadcast: The Dudley Boys overcame Edge and Christian, and Test and Albert in a three-way elimination tables match; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory overcame Lita; WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman overturned Perry Saturn; Crash Holly beat WWF European Champion William Regal to win the title; Chyna and WWF Intercontinental Champion Billy Gunn toppled Eddie Guerrero and Dean Malenko; Kane bested Chris Jericho; WWF World Tag Champions The Goodfather and Bull Buchanan overturned The Hardy Boys; The Undertaker dropped Chris Benoit; WWF World Champion Kurt Angle won a four-way over The Rock, Steve Austin, and Rikishi in a four-way.

5th May 2001: “WWF Insurrextion 2001”: London, England – 16,284 sellout
($840,054 live gate) (GBP£583,812)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
PPV Broadcast: Eddie Guerrero overcame Brian Christopher; Perry Saturn, Dean Malenko, and Terri defeated Hardcore, Crash, and Molly Holly; Bradshaw beat The Big Show; Edge and Christian downed The Dudley Boys, WWF European Champion Matt Hardy and Jeff Hardy, and X-Pac and Justin Credible in a four-way elimination match; Chris Benoit toppled Kurt Angle in a “best-of-three-falls” encounter. 2:0; Chris Jericho bested William Regal in a “Queen’s Cup” match; The Undertaker bested WWF World Tag Champions Triple H and Steve Austin in a handicap match.

3rd November 2001: “WWF Rebellion 2001”: Manchester, England – 15,612 sellout  (14,968 paid)
($753,773 live gate)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Dark match: Billy Gunn and Chuck Palumbo defeated Lance Storm and Justin Credible.
PPV Broadcast: WWF Intercontinental Champion Edge dropped WWF European Champion Christian in a cage match; Scotty 2 Hotty beat The Hurricane; The Big Show toppled Diamond Dallas Page; WCW World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned The Acolytes and The Hardy Boys in a three-way elimination match; William Regal overcame Tajiri; WCW World Champion Chris Jericho bested Kurt Angle; Lita and Torrie Wilson bested Molly Holly and Stacy Keibler. Trish Stratus was guest referee; WWF World Champion Steve Austin bested The Rock.

4th May 2002: “WWF Insurrextion 2002”: London, England – 9,308 sellout
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Dark match: Curt Hennig overcame Goldust.
PPV Broadcast: Rob Van Dam rolled over WWF Intercontinental Champion Eddie Guerrero by DQ; Trish Stratus and Jacquelyn toppled WWF Women’s Champion Jazz and Molly Holly; X-Pac pinned Bradshaw;  Booker T overturned WWF Hardcore Champion Steven Richards to win the title; The Hardy Boys beat Brock Lesnar and Shawn Stasiak; WWF European Champion Spike Dudley beat William Regal; Steve Austin bested The Big Show. Ric Flair was guest referee; Triple H rolled over The Undertaker.

26th October 2002: “WWF Rebellion 2002”: Manchester, England – 13,416 sellout
($764,281 live gate)
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Dark match: Bill DeMott overcame Shannon Moore.
PPV Broadcast: Booker T beat Matt Hardy; Billy Kidman and Torrie Wilson bested John Cena and Dawn Marie; Sho Funaki overturned Crash Holly; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Jamie Noble overcame Tajiri and Rey Mysterio in a three-way elimination match; D-Von Dudley and Ron Simmons overturned Val Venis and Chuck Palumbo; Rikishi pinned Albert in a “Kiss My Ass” match; WWE Tag Team Champions Kurt Angle and Chris Benoit toppled Eddie and Chavo Guerrero Jr; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar and Paul Heyman bested Edge in a handicap match.

7th June 2003: “WWE Insurrextion 2003”: Newcastle, England – 10,000
Shown on PPV in the UK via Sky Box Office. The event was not broadcast in the United States.
Dark matches: Maven bested Christian X; The Hurricane dropped Lance Storm.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Women’s Champion Jazz beat Trish Stratus; WWE Intercontinental Champion Christian downed Booker T; WWE World Tag Champions Rob Van Dam and Kane toppled Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree; Goldust overcame Rico; Bubba Ray, D-Von, and Spike Dudley beat Rodney Mack, Christopher Nowinski, and Teddy Long; Scott Steiner dropped Test; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Kevin Nash in a streetfight.
