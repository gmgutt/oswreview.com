WWF In Your House Results

WWF IN YOUR HOUSE RESULTS

14th May 1995: “WWF In Your House”: Syracuse, NY – 7,000 (3,500 paid)
($51,648 live gate)
Dark matches: Jean Pierre Lafitte beat Bob Holly; Bam Bam Bigelow toppled Tatanka; The Undertaker rolled over Kama.
Raw: (06/05): Davey Boy Smith battled Owen Hart to a draw (KOTR Qualifier).
PPV Broadcast: Bret Hart dropped Hakushi; Razor Ramon bested WWF Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett and The Roadie in a handicap match; Mabel overcame Adam Bomb (KOTR Qualifier); WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Yokozuna defeated The Smoking Gunns; Jerry Lawler toppled Bret Hart; WWF World Champion Diesel rolled over Sid Vicious by DQ.

23rd July 1995: “WWF In Your House II”: Nashville, TN – 6,482
($74,930 live gate)
Dark matches: Skip beat Aldo Montoya; Bret Hart dropped Jean Pierre Lafitte; The Undertaker overturned Kama in a casket match.
PPV Broadcast: The Roadie pinned The 1-2-3 Kid; Men on a Mission toppled Razor Ramon and Savio Vega; Bam Bam Bigelow bested Henry Godwinn; Shawn Michaels overcame WWF Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett to win the title; WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Yokozuna defeated Lex Luger and Davey Boy Smith; WWF World Champion Diesel rolled over Sid Vicious in a lumberjack match.

24th September 1995: “WWF In Your House III”: Saginaw, MI – 5,146
($62,392 live gate)
Dark matches: Fatu defeated Hunter Hearst Helmsley; Goldust beat Bob Holly; Ahmed Johnson (Tony Norris) dropped Skip; The Undertaker overturned Mabel.
PPV Broadcast: Savio Vega defeated Waylon Mercy; Sid Vicious pinned Henry Godwinn; Davey Boy Smith dropped Bam Bam Bigelow; Dean Douglas bested Razor Ramon; Bret Hart toppled Jean Pierre Lafitte; WWF World Champion Diesel and WWF Intercontinental Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over WWF World Tag Champions Yokozuna and Davey Boy Smith (a permitted substitute for Owen Hart) to ostensibly win the titles.

22nd October 1995: “WWF In Your House IV”: Winnipeg, MAN – 10,339 (9,000 paid)
($93,422 live gate)
Dark matches: Bob Holly beat Rad Radford; Henry Godwinn defeated Sid Vicious; Bret Hart bested Isaac Yankem; Owen Hart and Yokozuna dropped Savio Vega and Bam Bam Bigelow.
PPV Broadcast: Hunter Hearst Helmsley beat Fatu; WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns overturned Razor Ramon and The 1-2-3 Kid; Goldust bested Marty Jannetty; Yokozuna battled King Mabel to a double count-out; Razor Ramon pinned WWF Intercontinental Champion Dean Douglas to win the title; Davey Boy Smith rolled over WWF World Champion Diesel by DQ.

17th December 1995: “WWF In Your House  V”: Hersey, PA – 7,289
($100,000 live gate)
Dark matches: Savio Vega beat Bob Backlund; Goldust defeated Duke Droese; WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns, Hakushi and Barry Horowitz overcame Yokozuna, Isaac Yankem, Skip and Zip.
PPV Broadcast: WWF Intercontinental Champion Razor Ramon and Marty Jannetty bested The 1-2-3 Kid and Sid Vicious; Ahmed Johnson defeated Buddy Landel; Hunter Hearst Helmsley downed Henry Godwinn in an “Arkansas Hog Pen” match; Owen Hart toppled Diesel by DQ; The Undertaker dropped King Mabel in a casket match; WWF World Champion Bret Hart rolled over Davey Boy Smith.

18th February 1996: “WWF In Your House 6”: Louisville, KY – 5,500 sellout
($83,000 live gate)
Dark matches: The Godwinns beat Skip and Zip; Ahmed Johnson downed Isaac Yankem; The Undertaker overturned WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust by count-out.
Free for All: Jake Roberts pinned Tatanka.
PPV Broadcast: Razor Ramon dropped The 1-2-3 Kid in a “Crybaby match”; Hunter Hearst Helmsley overcame Duke Droese; Yokozuna bested Davey Boy Smith by DQ; Shawn Michaels toppled Owen Hart; WWF World Champion Bret Hart rolled over Diesel in a cage match.

28th April 1996: “WWF IYH 7: Good Friends, Better Enemies”: Omaha, NE – 9,563 sellout (8,273 paid)
($120,668 live gate)
Dark matches: Savio Vega beat Steve Austin; Hunter Hearst Helmsley pinned Marc Mero; The Undertaker bested Mankind.
Free For All: Marc Mero toppled The 1-2-3 Kid by DQ
PPV Broadcast: Davey Boy Smith and Owen Hart downed Jake Roberts and Ahmed Johnson; The Ultimate Warrior bested WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust by count-out; Vader toppled Razor Ramon; WWF World Tag Champions Skip and Zip overcame The Godwinns; WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over Diesel in a no holds barred encounter.

26th May 1996: “IYH: Beware of Dog”: Florence, SC – 6,000 (4,796 paid) (64% full)
($63,435 live gate)
Free for All: The Smoking Gunns overcame WWF World Tag Champions The Godwinns to win the titles; Bob Holly defeated Isaac Yankem.
Dark matches: Ahmed Johnson overturned Jerry Lawler; The Ultimate Warrior pinned Owen Hart.
Dark matches: Matches were originally scheduled for PPV broadcast but did not air due to a power cut in the arena. They were re-taped on 05/28: Savio Vega beat Steve Austin in a Carribean Strap match; Yokozuna toppled Vader; WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust bested The Undertaker in a casket match; Jake Roberts downed Justin Bradshaw.
PPV Broadcast: Marc Mero bested Hunter Hearst Helmsley; WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels battled Davey Boy Smith to a draw.

28th May 1996: “WWF Superstars TV Taping and WWF IYH: Beware of Dog II TV Taping”: North Charleston, SC – 4,500
($43,977 live gate)
Dark matches: Barry Horowitz beat Owen Hart by DQ; Ahmed Johnson pinned Owen Hart; The Undertaker toppled Mankind; WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust in a non-title match.
IYH Beware of Dog II: Savio Vega downed Steve Austin in a Carribean Strap match; Vader pinned Yokozuna; WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust bested The Undertaker in a casket match.

21st July 1996: “WWF IYH IX: International Incident”: Vancouver, BC – 14,804 (11,955 paid)
(CAN$214,985 live gate)
Jake Roberts was advertised for the show (vs. Mankind) but did not appear due to injury.
Free for All: Justin Bradshaw beat Savio Vega
PPV Broadcast: Skip and Zip toppled WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns in a non-title match; Mankind defeated Henry Godwinn; Steve Austin downed Marc Mero; The Undertaker toppled Goldust; Vader, Davey Boy Smith, and Owen Hart rolled over WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels, WWF Intercontinental Champion Ahmed Johnson, and Sid Vicious.

22nd September 1996: “WWF In Your House X: Mind Games”: Philadelphia, PA – 15,000 (11,969 paid)
($210,290 live gate)
Record paid attendance and live gate revenue for an IYH event.
Dark matches: Jake Roberts defeated Hunter Hearst Helmsley; Faarooq downed Marc Mero; Sid Vicious dropped Vader.
Free For All: Savio Vega toppled Marty Jannetty.
PPV Broadcast: Savio Vega downed Justin Bradshaw in a “caribbean strap” match; Jose Lothario pinned Jim Cornette; Owen Hart and Davey Boy Smith defeated WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns to win the titles; Mark Henry bested Jerry Lawler; The Undertaker toppled Goldust in a “Final Curtain” match (pinfalls only); WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over Mankind by DQ.

20th October 1996: “WWF In Your House XI: Buried Alive”: Indianapolis, IN – 9,649 (8,238 paid)
($135,605 live gate)
Dark matches: Barry Windham beat Justin Bradshaw; The Godwinns defeated Marty Jannetty and Leif Cassidy; WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over Goldust.
PPV Broadcast: Steve Austin bested Hunter Hearst Helmsley; WWF World Tag Champions Davey Boy Smith and Owen Hart defeated The Smoking Gunns; WWF Intercontinental Champion Marc Mero pinned Goldust; Sid Vicious toppled Vader; The Undertaker toppled Mankind in a “Buried Alive” match.

15th December 1996: “WWF In Your House XII: It’s Time”: West Palm Beach, FL – 5,708 sellout (4,581 paid)
($69,018 live gate)
Dark matches: Brakkus dropped Dr X (Tom Pritchard); Steve Austin bested Goldust; Shawn Michaels rolled over Mankind.
Free For All:  Rocky Maivia defeated Salvatore Sincere by DQ.
PPV Broadcast: Flash Funk pinned Leif Cassidy; WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Davey Boy Smith overturned Razor Ramon II and Diesel II; Marc Mero defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion Hunter Hearst Helmsley by count-out; The Undertaker toppled The Executioner in a “last man standing” match; WWF World Champion Sid Vicious dropped Bret Hart.

16th February 1997: “WWF In Your House: Final Four”: Chattanooga, TN – 6,399
($76,726 live gate)
Dark match: The Godwinns beat The Headbangers; Sid rolled over WWF World Champion Bret Hart by DQ.
PPV Broadcast: Marc Mero pinned Leif Cassidy; Farooq, Crush and Savio Vega overcame Goldust, Flash Funk and Bart Gunn; WWF Intercontinental Champion Rocky Maivia bested Hunter Hearst Helmsley; Doug Furnas and Phil LaFon toppled WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Davey Boy Smith by DQ; Bret Hart rolled over The Undertaker, Vader and Steve Austin in a “Final Four” elimination match to win the vacant WWF World Title.

20th April 1997: “WWF In Your House: Revenge of the Taker”: Rochester, NY – 6,477 sellout
($87,414 live gate)
Record live gate for that market.
Dark matches: Doug Furnas and Phil Lafon downed The Godwinns; Hunter Hearst Helmsley pinned Goldust.
Free for All: The Sultan beat Flash Funk.
PPV Broadcast: The Legion of Doom dropped WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Davey Boy Smith by DQ; Savio Vega defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion Rocky Maivia by count-out; Jesse Jammes pinned Rockabilly; WWF World Champion The Undertaker bested Mankind; Steve Austin downed Bret Hart by DQ.

11th May 1997: “WWF In Your House: A Cold Day in Hell”: Richmond, VA – 9,381 sellout (7,681 paid)
($116,547 live gate)
($55,000 merchandise sales)
Record live gate for that market
Dark match: The Legion of Doom dropped WWF World Tag Champions Owen Hart and Davey Boy Smith by DQ.
Free for All: Rockabilly bested Jesse Jammes.
PPV Broadcast: Hunter Hearst Helmsley bested Flash Funk; Mankind overturned Rocky Maivia; Ahmed Johnson toppled Crush; Ahmed Johnson downed Savio Vega by DQ; Faarooq pinned Ahmed Johnson; Ken Shamrock defeated Vader in a “no holds barred” match; WWF World Champion The Undertaker rolled over Steve Austin.

6th July 1997: “WWF IYH: Canadian Stampede”: Calgary, Alberta – 12,151 (10,974 paid)
($229,598 live gate)
($69,000 merchandise sales)
Free for All: The Godwinns beat The New Blackjacks.
PPV Broadcast: Mankind battled Triple H to a double count-out; The Great Sasuke toppled Taka Michinoku; WWF World Champion The Undertaker bested Vader; Bret Hart, WWF Intercontinental Champion Owen Hart, WWF European Champion Davey Boy Smith, Brian Pillman and Jim Neidhart rolled over Steve Austin, Ken Shamrock, Goldust and The Legion of Doom.

7th September 1997: “WWF IYH: Ground Zero”: Louisville, KY – 4,963 sellout
($82,228 live gate)
($44,184 merchandise sales)
Brian Pillman bested Goldust in a “career vs. Marlena’s services” encounter; Brian Christopher downed Scott Putski by stoppage; Savio Vega overcame Faarooq and Crush in a Triple Threat match; Max Mini beat El Torito; The Headbangers overturned Owen Hart and WWF European Champion Davey Boy Smith, The Godwinns and The Legion of Doom in a Fatal Four Way elimination match to win the titles;  WWF World Champion Bret Hart toppled The Patriot; The Undertaker battled Shawn Michaels to a no-contest.

5th October 1997: “WWF IYH: Badd Blood”: St Louis, MO – 21,151 sellout (17,404 paid)
($212,550 live gate)
($101,115 merchandise sales)
Brian Pillman was scheduled for the show in singles matches against Dude Love and Goldust, but had died earlier that day.
The Rock, D-Lo Brown and Kama beat The Legion of Doom in a handicap match; Max Mini and Nova overturned Tarantula and Mosaic; The Godwinns downed WWF World Tag Champions The Headbangers to win the titles; Owen Hart toppled Faarooq to win the vacant WWF Intercontinental Title (WWF Intercontinental Title Tournament – Final); Crush, Chainz, Skull and Eight-Ball dropped Savio Vega, Miguel Perez, Jose Estrada and Jesus Castillo; WWF World Champion Bret Hart and Davey Boy Smith bested The Patriot and Vader in a flag match; WWF European Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over The Undertaker in a “Hell-in-a-Cell” match.

7th December 1997: “WWF IYH: Degeneration X”: Springfield, MA – 6,358 sellout
($112,864 live gate)
($44,000 merchandise sales)
Taka Michinoku beat Brian Christopher to win the WWF Light Heavyweight Title (WWF Light Heavyweight Title Tournament – Final); Jesus Castillo, Miguel Perez and Jose Estrada bested Chainz, Skull and Eight-Ball; Butterbean downed Marc Mero by DQ in a “toughman” match; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg dropped The Legion of Doom by DQ; Triple H overturned Sgt Slaughter in a “Bootcamp” encounter; Jeff Jarrett bested The Undertaker by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Steve Austin toppled The Rock; Ken Shamrock rolled over WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels by DQ.

15th February 1998:  “WWF IYH: No Way Out”:  Houston, TX- 16,110 sellout (14,280 paid)
($241,992 live gate)
($166,343 merchandise sales)
The Headbangers defeated Marc Mero and TAFKA Goldust; WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku pinned Pantera; The Godwinns downed The Quebecers; Bradshaw dropped NWA North American Champion Jeff Jarrett by DQ; Ken Shamrock, Ahmed Johnson, Skull, Eight-Ball and Chainz overcame Faarooq, WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock, D-Lo Brown, Kama and Mark Henry in a “war-of-attrition” match; Kane bested Vader; Steve Austin, WWF European Champion Owen Hart, Cactus Jack and Terry Funk rolled over Triple H, Savio Vega and WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn.

26th April 1998: “WWF IYH: Unforgiven”: Greensboro, NC – 21,427 sellout (20,268 paid)
($341,270 live gate)
($165,572 merchandise sales)
Ken Shamrock, Faarooq and Steve Blackman defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock, D-Lo Brown and Mark Henry; WWF European Champion Triple H dropped Owen Hart. Chyna was suspended above the ring in a cage; NWA Tag Team Champions Bob Holly and Bart Gunn overcame Ricky Morton and Robert Gibson; Luna Vachon overcame Sable in an “evening gown” match; WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn overturned The Legion of Doom; The Undertaker bested Kane in an “inferno” match; Dude Love rolled over WWF World Champion Steve Austin by DQ.

31st May 1998: “WWF IYH: Over The Edge”: Milwaukee, WI – 9,822 sellout
($186,383 live gate)
($93,672 merchandise sales
The Legion of Doom defeated Skull and Eight-Ball; Jeff Jarrett downed Steve Blackman; Marc Mero pinned Sable; Mens Teioh, Sho Funaki and Dick Togo dropped Bradshaw and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku in a handicap match; WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock downed Faarooq; Kane bested Vader in a “Mask vs. Mask” encounter; Owen Hart, D-Lo Brown and Kama toppled WWF European Champion Triple H and WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over Dude Love in a “falls-count-anywhere, no-DQ” match. Vince McMahon was guest referee.

26th July 1998: “WWF IYH: Fully Loaded”: Fresno, CA – 9,855 sellout (8,835 paid)
($179,435 live gate)
($71,239 merchandise sales)
Val Venis beat Jeff Jarrett; WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown defeated X-Pac; Scorpio and Faarooq downed Terry Funk and Bradshaw; Mark Henry pinned Vader; Skull and Eight-Ball overcame The Legion of Doom; Owen Hart overturned Ken Shamrock in a “dungeon” match. Dan Severn was guest referee; Triple H battled WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock to a draw in a “best-of-three-falls” outing; Sable defeated Jackie in a bikini contest; WWF World Champion Steve Austin and The Undertaker bested WWF World Tag Champions Kane and Mankind to win the titles.

27th September 1998: “WWF IYH: Breakdown”: Hamilton, ONT – 17,405 sellout (16,158 paid)
(CAN$322,099 live gate)
(CAN$138,894 merchandise sales)
Owen Hart defeated Edge; Al Snow and Scorpio downed Brian Christopher and Scott Taylor; Marc Mero dropped Droz; Bradshaw overcame Vader in a “falls-count-anywhere” match; D-Lo Brown bested Gangrel; The Rock downed Ken Shamrock and Mankind in a Triple Threat cage match; Val Venis overturned Dustin Runnels; WWF European Champion X-Pac and WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg dropped Jeff Jarrett and Southern Justice; The Undertaker and Kane toppled WWF World Champion Steve Austin by double pin in a Triple Threat match.

18th October 1998: “WWF IYH: Judgment Day”: Chicago, IL – 18,153 sellout (16,458 paid)
($357,050 live gate)
($144,930 merchandise sales)
Heat: (10/18): Steve Blackman beat Bradshaw; Giant Silva, Kurrgan and Golga downed Jose Estrada, Miguel Perez and Jesus Castillo; The Godfather defeated Faarooq; Scorpio dropped Jeff Jarrett.
PPV Broadcast: Al Snow beat Marc Mero; The Legion of Doom and Droz defeated Skull, Eight-Ball and Paul Ellering; Christian pinned WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku to win the title; Goldust bested Val Venis; X-Pac overcame WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown to win the title; The Headbangers overturned WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Ken Shamrock toppled Mankind; Mark Henry rolled over The Rock; The Undertaker battled Kane to a no-contest. Steve Austin was guest referee.

13th December 1998: “WWF In Your House: Rock Bottom”: Vancouver, BC – 17,677 sellout (16,891 paid)
(CAN$416,032 live gate)
(CAN$156,463 merchandise sales)
D-Lo Brown and Mark Henry beat Val Venis and The Godfather; The Headbangers overcame Golga and Kurrgan; Steve Blackman defeated Owen Hart by count-out; Gangrel, Edge and Christian downed Al Snow, Bob Holly and Scorpio; Goldust bested Jeff Jarrett by reverse decision-DQ; WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn dropped WWF Intercontinental Champion Ken Shamrock and WWF Hardcore Champion The Big Bossman; Mankind toppled WWF World Champion The Rock by stoppage; Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker in a “Buried Alive” match.
