WWE Summerslam Results

WWF/E SUMMERSLAM RESULTS

29th August 1988: “WWF Summerslam 1988”: New York, NY (MSG) – 20,000 sellout
($355,345 live gate)
PPV Broadcast: Davey Boy Smith and The Dynamite Kid battled Jacques and Raymond Rougeau to a draw; Bad News Brown rolled over Ken Patera; Rick Rude beat The Junkyard Dog by DQ; The Powers of Pain defeated Boris Zhukoff and Nikolai Volkoff; The Ultimate Warrior pinned WWF Intercontinental Champion The Honkytonk Man to win the title; Dino Bravo downed Don Muraco; WWF World Tag Champions Demolition toppled Bret Hart and Jim Neidhart; The Big Bossman rolled over Koko B Ware; Jake Roberts overturned Hercules; Hulk Hogan and WWF World Champion Randy Savage bested Ted Dibiase and Andre the Giant.

28th August 1989: “WWF Summerslam 1989”: East Rutherford, NJ – 20,000 sellout
($350,000 live gate)
Dark match: Dino Bravo downed Koko B Ware.
PPV Broadcast: WWF World Tag Champions Arn Anderson and Tully Blanchard defeated Bret Hart and Jim Neidhart in a non-title match; Dusty Rhodes overturned The Honkytonk Man; Curt Hennig pinned The Red Rooster; Rick Martel, Jacques Rougeau, and Raymond Rougeau downed Tito Santana, Shawn Michaels and Marty Jannetty;  The Ultimate Warrior toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Rick Rude to win the title; Jim Duggan and Demolition rolled over Andre The Giant, The Big Bossman, and Akeem; Hercules bested Greg Valentine by DQ; Ted Dibiase bested Jimmy Snuka by count-out; WWF World Champion Hulk Hogan and Brutus Beefcake bested Randy Savage and Zeus.

27th August 1990: “WWF Summerslam 1990”: Philadelphia, PA – 19,304 sellout (18,703 paid)
($338,452 live gate)
Dark match: Shane Douglas overturned Buddy Rose.
PPV Broadcast:  Paul Roma and Hercules overturned Shawn Michaels and Marty Jannetty; Kerry Von Erich pinned WWF Intercontinental Champion Curt Hennig to win the title; Sensational Sherri defeated Sapphire by forfeit; The Warlord beat Tito Santana; Bret Hart and Jim Neidhart dropped WWF World Tag Champions Demolition (Crush and Smash) in a “best-of-three-falls” encounter to win the titles; Jake Roberts overturned Bad News Brown by DQ; Jim Duggan and Nikolai Volkoff defeated The Orient Express; Randy Savage bested Dusty Rhodes; Hulk Hogan rolled over Earthquake by count-out; WWF World Champion The Ultimate Warrior toppled Rick Rude in a cage match.

26th August 1991: “WWF Summerslam 1991”: New York, NY (MSG) – 20,000 sellout
($445,000 live gate)
Dark match: Koko B Ware beat Kato.
PPV Broadcast: Ricky Steamboat, Davey Boy Smith, and Kerry Von Erich bested The Warlord, Hercules, and Paul Roma; Bret Hart downed WWF Intercontinental Champion Curt Hennig to win the title; The Natural Disasters defeated The Bushwhackers; Virgil pinned Million Dollar Champion Ted Dibiase to win the title; The Big Bossman dropped The Mountie in a “Jailhouse” match; The Legion of Doom overturned WWF World Tag Champions The Nasty Boys in a no DQ, no count-out match to win the titles; IRS beat Greg Valentine; WWF World Champion Hulk Hogan and The Ultimate Warrior rolled over Sgt Slaughter, Colonel Mustafa, and General Adnan in a handicap match. Sid Justice was guest referee.

29th August 1992: “WWF Summerslam 1992”: London, England – 79,127 sellout (78,927 paid)
($2,200,000 live gate)
($1,450,000 merchandise sales)
Prime Time: (09/14): Jim Duggan and The Bushwhackers overturned The Mountie and The Nasty Boys.
Prime Time: (09/21): Papa Shango bested Tito Santana.
Prime Time: (09/28): Tatanka pinned The Berzerker.
PPV Broadcast: (08/31): The Legion of Doom dropped Ted Dibiase and IRS; Nailz defeated Virgil; Shawn Michaels battled Rick Martel to a double count-out; WWF World Tag Champions The Natural Disasters overcame The Beverly Brothers; Crush downed Repo Man; The Ultimate Warrior bested WWF World Champion Randy Savage by count-out; The Undertaker toppled Kamala by DQ; Davey Boy Smith rolled over WWF Intercontinental Champion Bret Hart to win the title.

30th August 1993: “WWF Summerslam 1993”: Auburn Hills, MI – 23,594 sellout
($215,000 live gate)
Dark match: Owen Hart defeated Barry Horowitz.
PPV Broadcast: Razor Ramon overcame Ted Dibiase; WWF World Tag Champions The Steiners defeated The Heavenly Bodies; WWF Intercontinental Champion Shawn Michaels downed Curt Hennig by count-out; IRS pinned The 1-2-3 Kid; Bret Hart dropped Doink by DQ; Jerry Lawler toppled Bret Hart by reverse decision-DQ; Ludvig Borga toppled Marty Jannetty; The Undertaker bested The Giant Gonzalez in a “Rest in Peace” match; Tatanka and The Smoking Gunns defeated Bam Bam Bigelow and The Headshrinkers; Lex Luger rolled over WWF World Champion Yokozuna by count-out.

29th August 1994: “WWF Summerslam 1994”: Chicago, IL – 23,000 sellout (19,500 paid)
($386,435 live gate)
Dark match: Adam Bomb beat Kwang
PPV Broadcast: Bam Bam Bigelow and IRS downed The Headshrinkers by DQ; WWF Women’s Champion Alundra Blayze dropped Bull Nakano; Razor Ramon overturned WWF Intercontinental Champion Diesel to win the title; Tatanka downed Lex Luger; Jeff Jarrett bested Mabel; WWF World Champion Bret Hart rolled over Owen Hart in a cage match; The Undertaker (Mark Callaway) pinned The Undertaker (Brian Lee).

27th August 1995: “WWF Summerslam 1995”: Pittsburgh, PA – 18,062 sellout (17,000 paid)
($353,705 live gate)
Hakushi pinned The 1-2-3 Kid; Hunter Hearst Helmsley downed Bob Holly; The Smoking Gunns defeated Jacob and Eli Blu; Barry Horowitz defeated Skip; Bertha Faye beat WWF Women’s Champion Alundra Blayze to win the title; The Undertaker bested Kama in a casket match; Bret Hart toppled Isaac Yankem by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Shawn Michaels overturned Razor Ramon in a ladder match; WWF World Champion Diesel rolled over King Mabel.

18th August 1996: “WWF Summerslam 1996”: Cleveland, OH – 17,000 (14,926 paid)
($413,168 live gate)
Free for All: Steve Austin beat Yokozuna.
PPV Broadcast: Owen Hart defeated Savio Vega; WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns overcame The Godwinnns, Skip and Zip, and Marty Jannetty and Leif Cassidy in a four-way; Sid Vicious bested Davey Boy Smith; Goldust toppled Marc Mero; Jerry Lawler downed Jake Roberts; Mankind overcame The Undertaker in a “boiler room” brawl; WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over Vader.

3rd August 1997: “WWF Summerslam 1997”: East Rutherford, NJ – 20,213 sellout (17,361 paid)
Set new gate record for Continental Airlines Arena
($523,154 live gate)
($202,500 merchandise sales)
Mankind defeated Hunter Hearst Helmsley in a cage match; Goldust downed Brian Pillman; The Legion of Doom toppled The Godwinns; WWF European Champion Davey Boy Smith bested Ken Shamrock by DQ; Savio Vega, Miguel Perez, Jesus Castillo and Jose Estrada overcame Crush, Skull, Eight-Ball and Chainz; Steve Austin bested WWF Intercontinental Champion Owen Hart to win the title; Bret Hart rolled over WWF World Champion The Undertaker to win the title. Shawn Michaels was guest referee.

30th August 1998: “WWF Summerslam 1998”: New York, NY (MSG) – 19,066 sellout (15,274 paid)
($764,165 live gate)
($209,000 merchandise sales)
MSG Theatre: 2,522 (1,816 paid) ($32,688 live gate)
WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown dropped Val Venis by DQ; Kurrgan, Golga and The Giant Silva overcame WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku, Mens Teioh, Sho Funaki and Dick Togo; X-Pac downed Jeff Jarrett in a “Hair vs. Hair” match; Edge and Sable bested Marc Mero and Jacquelyn; Ken Shamrock toppled Owen Hart in a “Lion’s Den” match; Billy Gunn and Road Dogg overturned WWF World Tag Champion Mankind in a handicap “falls-count-anywhere” match to win the titles; Triple H defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock in a ladder match to win the title; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker.

22nd August 1999: “WWF Summerslam 1999”: Minneapolis, MN –  17,370 sellout (15,973 paid)
($557,129 live gate)
($168,456 merchandise sales)
Jeff Jarrett defeated WWF Intercontinental and European Champion D-Lo Brown to win the titles; The Acolytes beat Matt and Jeff Hardy, Edge and Christian, Mideon and Viscera, Albert and Droz, and Hardcore and Crash Holly in a tag team turmoil match; Al Snow pinned WWF Hardcore Champion The Big Bossman to win the title; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory bested Tori; Ken Shamrock dropped Steve Blackman in a “Lion’s Den” match; Test pinned Shane McMahon in a “Greenwich” streetfight; The Undertaker and The Big Show toppled WWF World Tag Champions Kane and X-Pac to win the titles; The Rock rolled over Billy Gunn in a “Kiss My Ass” match; Mankind downed WWF World Champion Steve Austin and Triple H in a Triple Threat match to win the title. Jesse Ventura was the guest referee.

27th August 2000: “WWF Summerslam 2000”: Raleigh, NC – 17,002 sellout (15,603 paid)
($1,151,940 live gate)
($147,815 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Steven Richards, Bull Buchanon, and The Goodfather beat Rikishi, Brian Christopher, and Scotty 2 Hotty; X-Pac pinned Road Dogg; Eddie Guerrero and Chyna defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion Val Venis and Trish Stratus. By virtue of a pre-match stipulation, Chyna was awarded the WWF Intercontinental Title; Jerry Lawler beat Tazz; Steve Blackman downed WWF Hardcore Champion Shane McMahon to win the title; Chris Benoit overcame Chris Jericho in a “best-of-three-falls” match. 2:1; WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian downed The Dudley Boys and The Hardy Boys in a three-way TLC match; The Kat overcame Terri in a “Stink Face” match; The Undertaker battled Kane to a no-contest; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over Kurt Angle and Triple H in a three-way.

19th August 2001: “WWF Summerslam 2001”: San Jose, CA – 15,293 sellout (14,087 paid)
($907,105 live gate)
($162,887 merchandise sales)
Heat: (08/19):  Lita, Molly Holly, and Jacquelyn toppled Ivory, Stacy Keibler, and Torrie Wilson.

24th August 2003: “WWE Summerslam 2003”: Phoenix, AZ – 16,113 sellout

17th August 2008: “WWE Summerslam 2008”: Indianapolis, IN – 12,480 sellout
($802,810 live gate)
Dark match: The Big Show beat Bam Neely.
PPV Broadcast: MVP defeated Jeff Hardy; Santino Marella and Beth Phoenix downed WWE Intercontinental Champion Kofi Kingston and WWE Women’s Champion Mickie James in a “Winner Takes All” encounter. By virtue of a pre-match stipulation, Marella won the IC title and Phoenix won the women’s title; Matt Hardy dropped ECW Champion Mark Henry by DQ; World Heavyweight Champion CM Punk bested JBL; WWE World Champion Triple H pinned The Great Khali; Batista toppled John Cena; The Undertaker rolled over Edge in a “Hell in a Cell” match.

($1,008,150 live gate)

Triple H was guest referee; Alberto Del Rio pinned WWE World Champion CM Punk to win the title.

($1,000,000 live gate)

17th August 2014: “WWE Summerslam 2014”: Los Angeles, CA – 14,000 sellout
Shown on PPV/WWE Network
Pre-show: Rob Van Dam pinned Cesaro.
PPV Broadcast: Dolph Ziggler beat WWE Intercontinental Champion The Miz to win the title; Paige downed WWE Divas Champion AJ Lee to win the title; Rusev overturned Jack Swagger by stoppage in a flag match; Seth Rollins dropped Dean Ambrose in a lumberjack match; Bray Wyatt toppled Chris Jericho; Stephanie McMahon bested Brie Bella; Roman Reigns pinned Randy Orton; Brock Lesnar rolled over WWE World Heavyweight Champion John Cena to win the title.


23rd August 2015: “WWE Summerslam 2015”: Brooklyn, NY – 13,589 sellout
($1,300,000 live gate)
Shown live on PPV/WWE Network
Sheamus rolled over Randy Orton; Big E & Kofi Kingston won a four-way over WWE World Tag Champions Darren Young & Titus O’Neil, Los Matadores and The Lucha Dragons to win the titles; Dolph Ziggler battled Rusev to a double count-out; Neville & Stephen Amell beat King Barrett & Stardust; WWE Intercontinental Champion Ryback beat The Big Show and The Miz in a three-way; Dean Ambrose & Roman Reigns rolled over Bray Wyatt & Luke Harper; WWE World Heavyweight Champion Seth Rollins beat WWE US Champion John Cena to win the title; Becky Lynch, Charlotte & Paige won a three-way elimination match over Alicia Fox, Brie Bella & Nikki Bella, and Naomi, Sasha Banks & Tamina; Kevin Owens rolled over Cesaro; The Undertaker overturned Brock Lesnar by stoppage.

21st August 2016: “WWE Summerslam 2016”: Brooklyn, NY – 13,400 sellout
($1,506,514 live gate)
Shown live on PPV/WWE Network
Kick-off Show: Jason Jordan, Chad Gable, Jimmy Uso, Jey Uso, Mojo Rawley and Zack Ryder rolled over Konnor, Fandango, Tyler Breeze, Aiden English, Simon Gotch and Viktor; Sami Zayn and Neville downed Bubba Ray Dudley and D-Von Dudley; Sheamus overturned Cesaro (Best of 7 Series – Match #1).
PPV Broadcast: Chris Jericho and Kevin Owens overturned Enzo Amore and Big Cass; Charlotte downed WWE Women’s Champion Sasha Banks to win the title; WWE Intercontinental Champion The Miz defeated Apollo Crews; AJ Styles overturned John Cena; Karl Anderson and Luke Gallows overturned WWE Tag Champions Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods by DQ; WWE World Heavyweight Champion Dean Ambrose defeated Dolph Ziggler; Nikki Bella, Alexa Bliss and Natalya beat Carmella, Becky Lynch and Naomi; Finn Balor rolled over Seth Rollins to become the first WWE Universal Champion; Brock Lesnar rolled over Randy Orton by stoppage.
