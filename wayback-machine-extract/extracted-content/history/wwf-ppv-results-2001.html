WWF PPV Results 2001

WWF PPV RESULTS (2001)

See also: WWF PPV Statistics 2001

21st January 2001: “WWF Royal Rumble 2001”: New Orleans, LA – 16,056 sellout  (13,481 paid)
($666,700 live gate)
($93,253 merchandise sales)
Heat: (01/21): D-Lo Brown and Chaz defeated Taka Michinoku and Sho Funaki.
PPV Broadcast: The Dudley Boys dropped WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian to win the titles; Chris Jericho bested WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Benoit in a ladder match to win the title; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory toppled Chyna; WWF World Champion Kurt Angle rolled over Triple H; Steve Austin won the 30-man Royal Rumble, last eliminating Kane.

25th February 2001: “WWF No Way Out 2001”: Las Vegas, NV – 15,223 sellout (13,119 paid)
($683,955 live gate)
($129,478 merchandise sales)
Heat: (02/25): Rikishi dropped Matt Hardy by DQ.
PPV Broadcast: The Big Show beat WWF Hardcore Champion Raven to win the title; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho defeated Chris Benoit, Eddie Guerrero, and X-Pac in a four-way; Stephanie McMahon pinned Trish Stratus; Triple H toppled Steve Austin in a “Three Stages of Hell” encounter; Steven Richards downed Jerry Lawler; WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys bested Edge and Christian, and The Undertaker and Kane in a three-way tables match; The Rock rolled over WWF World Champion Kurt Angle to win the title.

1st April 2001: “WWE Wrestlemania X-7”: Houston, TX – 67,925 sellout (62,885 paid)
($3,530,905 live gate)
($1,111,343 merchandise sales)
Heat: (04/01): Justin Credible and X-Pac beat Steve Blackman and Brian Christopher.
PPV Broadcast: WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho bested William Regal; Tazz and The Acolytes overcame Bull Buchanon, The Goodfather, and Val Venis; Kane overturned WWF Hardcore Champion Raven and The Big Show to win the title; Eddie Guerrero beat WWF European Champion Test to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled Chris Benoit; Chyna pinned WWF Women’s Champion Ivory to win the title; Shane McMahon bested Vince McMahon in a streetfight; Edge and Christian downed WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys and The Hardy Boys in a “TLC” encounter to win the titles; The Iron Sheik won a “gimmick” battle royal; The Undertaker toppled Triple H; Steve Austin rolled over WWF World Champion The Rock to win the title in a no-DQ match.

29th April 2001: “WWE Backlash 2001”: Chicago, IL – 15,592 sellout (14,751 paid)
($831,510 live gate)
($143,908 merchandise sales)
Heat: (04/29): Jerry Lynn bested WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Lita beat Molly Holly.
PPV Broadcast: X-Pac, Justin Credible, and Albert overcame Bubba Ray, D-Von, and Spike Dudley; WWF Hardcore Champion Rhyno overturned Raven; William Regal pinned Chris Jericho in a “Dutchess of Queensbury Rules” match; Chris Benoit toppled Kurt Angle in a 30-minute “Ultimate Submission” match. 4:3; Shane McMahon bested The Big Show in a “Last Man Standing” match; WWF European Champion Matt Hardy won a three-way over Christian and Eddie Guerrero; WWF World Champion Steve Austin and WWF Intercontinental Champion Triple H rolled over WWF World Tag Champions The Undertaker and Kane to win the titles.

20th May 2001: “WWF Judgment Day 2001”:Sacramento, CA – 13,623 sellout
($674,855 live gate)
Heat: (05/20): Raven bested Val Venis; Hardcore and Crash Holly downed Taka Michinoku and Sho Funaki.
PPV Broadcast: William Regal pinned Rikishi; Kurt Angle toppled Chris Benoit in a “Three Stages of Hell” encounter; WWF Hardcore Champion Rhyno overturned The Big Show and Test in a three-way; WWF Women’s Champion Chyna pinned Lita; Kane toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion and WWF Tag Team Champion Triple H in a chain match; Chris Jericho and Chris Benoit won a “Tag Team Turmoil” match over beat Edge and Christian, Justin Credible and X-Pac, The Hardy Boys, The Acolytes, The Dudley Boys, and Dean Malenko and Perry Saturn; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker in a “no holds barred” match.

24th June 2001: “WWF King of the Ring 2001”: East Rutherford, NJ – 16,024 sellout
($1,132,375 live gate)
($131,673 merchandise sales)
Heat: (06/24): WWF European Champion Matt Hardy beat Justin Credible.
PPV Broadcast: Kurt Angle toppled Christian (KOTR 2001 – Semi-Final); Edge bested Rhyno (KOTR 2001 – Semi-Final); WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned Spike Dudley and WWF Intercontinental Champion Kane; Edge dropped Kurt Angle (KOTR 2001 – Final); WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Jeff Hardy beat X-Pac; Kurt Angle toppled Shane McMahon in a streetfight; WWF World Champion Steve Austin bested Chris Jericho and Chris Benoit in a three-way.

22nd July 2001: “WWF Invasion”: Cleveland, OH – 17,019 sellout  (15,535 paid)
($848,060 live gate)
($146,452 merchandise sales)
Heat: (07/22): Chavo Guerrero Jr overturned Scotty 2 Hotty.
PPV Broadcast: Edge and Christian downed Mike Awesome and Lance Storm; Earl Hebner beat Nick Patrick. Mick Foley was guest referee; WWF World Tag Champions The Acolytes overturned WCW World Tag Champions Sean O’Haire and Chuck Palumbo in a non-title match; WCW Cruiserweight Champion Billy Kidman bested WWF Light Heavyweight Champion X-Pac in a non-title match; Raven bested William Regal; Hugh Morrus, Shawn Stasiak, and Chris Kanyon toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Albert, The Big Show, and Billy Gunn; Tajiri beat Tazz; Rob Van Dam rolled over WWF Hardcore Champion Jeff Hardy to win the title; Lita and Trish Stratus overcame Torrie Wilson and Stacy Keibler in a “bra and panties” match. Mick Foley was guest referee; WCW World Champion Booker T, Rhyno, Diamond Dallas Page, and The Dudley Boys rolled over WWF World Champion Steve Austin, Chris Jericho, Kurt Angle, The Undertaker, and Kane.

19th August 2001: “WWF Summerslam 2001”: San Jose, CA – 15,293 sellout (14,087 paid)
($907,105 live gate)
($162,887 merchandise sales)
Heat: (08/19):  Lita, Molly Holly, and Jacquelyn toppled Ivory, Stacy Keibler, and Torrie Wilson.
PPV Broadcast: Edge toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Lance Storm to win the title; Test and The Dudley Boys overturned Spike Dudley and The Acolytes; WCW Cruiserweight Champion X-Pac pinned WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Tajiri to win the title; Chris Jericho bested Rhyno; Rob Van Dam dropped WWF Hardcore Champion Jeff Hardy in a ladder match to win the title; WCW World Tag Champions The Undertaker and Kane rolled over WWF World Tag Champions Diamond Dallas Page and WCW US Champion Kanyon in a cage match to win the titles; Kurt Angle toppled WWF World Champion Steve Austin by DQ; The Rock pinned WCW World Champion Booker T to win the title.

23rd September 2001: “WWF Unforgiven 2001”: Pittsburgh, PA – 13,855 (12,887 paid)
($640,115 live gate)
($50,702 merchandise sales)
Heat: (09/23): Billy Gunn bested Tommy Dreamer.
PPV Broadcast: WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned The Big Show and Spike Dudley, Lance Storm and WWF European Champion The Hurricane, and The Hardy Boys in a four-way elimination match; Perry Saturn bested Raven; Christian downed WWF Intercontinental Champion Edge to win the title; WCW World Tag Champions The Undertaker and Kane toppled Kronik; WWF Hardcore Champion Rob Van Dam rolled over Chris Jericho; WCW World Champion The Rock defeated Booker T and Shane McMahon in a handicap match; Rhyno overturned WCW US Champion Tajiri to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled WWF World Champion Steve Austin to win the title.

21st October 2001: “WWF No Mercy 2001”: St Louis, MO – 15,647 (14,486 paid)
($762,255 live gate)
($123,486 merchandise sales)
Heat: (10/21): The Acolytes overturned Hugh Morrus and Kanyon; WCW Cruiserweight Champion Billy Kidman bested Scotty 2 Hotty.
PPV Broadcast: WCW World Tag Champions The Hardy Boys beat WWF European Champion The Hurricane and Lance Storm; Test overcame Kane; Torrie Wilson bested Stacy Keibler; Edge dropped WWF Intercontinental Champion Christian in a ladder match to win the title; WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned The Big Show and Tajiri; The Undertaker bested Booker T; Chris Jericho bested WCW World Champion The Rock to win the title; WWF World Champion Steve Austin bested Kurt Angle and WWF Hardcore Champion Rob Van Dam in a no-DQ, three-way match.

18th November 2001: “WWF Survivor Series 2001”: Greensboro, NC – 10,142
($594,720 live gate)
($91,409 merchandise sales)
Heat: (11/18): Raven, Lance Storm, and Justin Credible toppled Albert, Scotty 2 Hotty, and Spike Dudley.
PPV Broadcast: WWF European Champion Christian downed Al Snow; William Regal pinned Tajiri; WCW US Champion Edge toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Test to unify the titles; WCW World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned WWF World Tag Champions The Hardy Boys in a cage match to unify the titles; Test won an “immunity” battle royal; Trish Stratus overcame Lita, Jacquelyn, Ivory, Molly Holly, and Jazz in a six-way to win the WWF Women’s Title; Team WWF (WCW World Champion The Rock, Chris Jericho, The Big Show, Kane, and The Undertaker) rolled over Team Alliance (WWF World Champion Steve Austin, Shane McMahon, Kurt Angle, Booker T, and WWF Hardcore Champion Rob Van Dam) in a “Winner Takes All”, “Survivor Series  elimination match.

9th December 2001: “WWE Vengeance 2001”: San Diego, CA – 11,800 sellout  (10,699 paid)
($550,580 live gate)
($100,696 merchandise sales)
Heat: (12/09): The Acolytes overturned Chuck Palumbo and Billy Gunn.
PPV Broadcast: Scotty 2 Hotty and Albert overcame Test and WWF European Champion Christian;; WWF Intercontinental Champion Edge toppled William Regal; Jeff Hardy dropped Matt Hardy. Lita was guest referee; WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys beat Kane and The Big Show; The Undertaker bested WWF Hardcore Champion Rob Van Dam to win the title; WWF Women’s Champion Trish Stratus overcame Jacquelyn; WWF World Champion Steve Austin bested Kurt Angle; Chris Jericho toppled WCW World Champion The Rock to win the title; WCW World Champion Chris Jericho rolled over WWF World Champion Steve Austin to unify the titles.
