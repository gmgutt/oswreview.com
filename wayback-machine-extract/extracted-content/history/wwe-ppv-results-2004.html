WWE PPV Results 2004

WWE PPV RESULTS (2004)

See also: WWE PPV Statistics 2004

25th January 2004: “WWE Royal Rumble 2004”: Philadelphia, PA – 17,289 sellout
($1,085,000 live gate)
Heat: (01/25): Victoria beat WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly in a non-title match.
PPV Broadcast: WWE World Tag Champions Ric Flair and Batista overturned The Dudley Boys in a tables match; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio dropped Jamie Noble; Eddie Guerrero overcame Chavo Guerrero Jr; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped Hardcore Holly; Shawn Michaels battled World Heavyweight Champion Triple H to a draw in a “last man standing” match; Chris Benoit won the 30-man Royal Rumble match.

15th February 2004: “WWE No Way Out 2004”: San Francisco, CA – 11,000 sellout (9,000 paid)
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (02/15): Tajiri, Akio, and Sakoda defeated Ultimo Dragon, Billy Kidman, and Paul London.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Team Champions Scotty 2 Hotty and Rikishi defeated Shaniqua, Doug and Danny Basham in a handicap match; Jamie Noble toppled Nidia; Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas downed The Acolytes; Hardcore Holly dropped Rhyno; Chavo Guerrero Jr beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled WWE US Champion The Big Show and John Cena in a three-way; Eddie Guerrero overcame WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar to win the title.

14th March 2004: “WWE Wrestlemania XX”: New York, NY (MSG) – 19,689 sellout
($2,401,000 live gate)
PPV Broadcast: John Cena bested WWE US Champion The Big Show to win the title; WWE World Tag Champions Rob Van Dam and Booker T won a four-way over  Mark Jindrak and Garrison Cade,  The Dudley Boys, and Rene Dupree and Rob Conway; Christian downed Chris Jericho; WWE Intercontinental Champion Randy Orton, Ric Flair, and Batista overturned The Rock and Mick Foley in a handicap match; Torrie Wilson and Sable toppled Stacy Keibler and Jackie Gayda in a “Playboy Evening Gown” match; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Chavo Guerrero Jr won a gauntlet match over Nunzio, Shannon Moore, Rey Mysterio, Sho Funaki, Ultimo Dragon, Jamie Noble, Billy Kidman, Tajiri, and Akio; Bill Goldberg pinned Brock Lesnar; WWE Tag Team Champions Rikishi and Scotty 2 Hotty won a four-way over  The Acolytes, Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas, and Doug and Danny Basham; WWE Women’s Champion Victoria toppled Molly Holly; WWE World Champion Eddie Guerrero overcame Kurt Angle; The Undertaker bested Kane; Chris Benoit toppled World Heavyweight Champion Triple H and Shawn Michaels in a three-way to win the title.

18th April 2004: “WWE Backlash 2004”: Edmonton, Alberta – 13,000
($483,000 live gate)
Heat: (04/18): Val Venis overturned Matt Hardy.
PPV Broadcast: Shelton Benjamin bested Ric Flair; Jonathan Coachman defeated Tajiri; Chris Jericho toppled Christian and Trish Stratus in a handicap match; WWE Women’s Champion Victoria beat Lita; WWE Intercontinental Champion Randy Orton rolled over Mick Foley in a “falls count anywhere, no holds barred” match; The Hurricane and Rosey beat Rob Conway and Sylvain Grenier; Edge toppled Kane; World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit won a three-way over Triple H and Shawn Michaels.

16th May 2004: “WWE Judgment Day 2004”: Los Angeles, CA – 18,722 sellout  (13,500 paid)
($813,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/16):  Mark Jindrak defeated Sho Funaki.
PPV Broadcast: Rey Mysterio and Rob Van Dam rolled over The Dudley Boys; Torrie Wilson bested Dawn Marie; Mordecai pinned Scotty 2 Hotty; WWE Tag Team Champions Charlie Haas and Rico overcame Hardcore Holly and Billy Gunn; Chavo Guerrero Jr beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Jacquelyn to win the title; WWE US Champion John Cena bested Rene Dupree; The Undertaker pinned Booker T; John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over WWE World Champion Eddie Guerrero by DQ.

13th June 2004: “WWE Bad Blood 2004”: Columbus, OH – 9,000 (7,500 paid)
($494,000 live gate)
Heat: (06/13): Batista overturned Maven.
PPV Broadcast: World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit and Edge dropped WWE World Tag Champions Rob Conway and Sylvain Grenier by DQ; Chris Jericho bested Tyson Tomko; WWE Intercontinental Champion Randy Orton rolled over Shelton Benjamin; Trish Stratus won a four-way over Lita, Gail Kim, and WWE Women’s Champion Victoria to win the title; Eugene overcame Jonathan Coachman; World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit toppled Kane; Triple H rolled over Shawn Michaels in a “Hell in a Cell” match.

27th June 2004: “WWE Great American Bash 2004”: Norfolk, VA – 6,500 (5,500 paid)
($320,000 live gate)
Heat: (06/27): Spike Dudley beat Jamie Noble.
PPV Broadcast: WWE US Champion John Cena bested Booker T, Rene Dupree, and Rob Van Dam in a four-way elimination match; Luther Reigns pinned Charlie Haas; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio dropped Chavo Guerrero Jr; Kenzo Suzuki toppled Billy Gunn; Sable overcame Torrie Wilson; Mordecai pinned Hardcore Holly; John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over WWE World Champion Eddie Guerrero in a bullrope match to win the title; The Undertaker bested WWE Tag Team Champions The Dudley Boys in a non-title handicap match.

11th July 2004: “WWE Vengeance 2004”: Hartford, CT – 7,000
($370,000 live gate)
Heat: (07/11): Tyson Tomko beat Val Venis.
PPV Broadcast: Tajiri and Rhyno defeated Garrison Cade and Jonathan Coachman; Batista overturned Chris Jericho; WWE World Tag Champions Rob Conway and Sylvain Grenier dropped Eugene and Ric Flair by DQ; Matt Hardy beat Kane in a no-DQ match; Edge toppled WWE Intercontinental Champion Randy Orton to win the title; Victoria toppled Molly Holly; World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit rolled over Triple H.

15th August 2004: “WWE Summerslam 2004”: Toronto, ONT – 17,640
($1,000,000 live gate)
Dark match: Rob Van Dam rolled over Rene Dupree.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Cruiserweight Champion Spike Dudley and The Dudley Boys overturned Rey Mysterio and WWE Tag Team Champions Billy Kidman and Paul London; Kane toppled Matt Hardy; John Cena bested WWE US Champion Booker T in a non-title match (Best of Five Series – match #1); WWE Intercontinental Champion Edge dropped Chris Jericho and Batista in a three-way; Kurt Angle toppled Eddie Guerrero; Triple H rolled over Eugene; WWE World Champion John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over The Undertaker by DQ; Randy Orton rolled over World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit to win the title.

12th September 2004: “WWE Unforgiven 2004”: Portland, OR – 10,000
($675,000 live gate)
Heat: (09/12): Maven beat Rodney Mack.
PPV Broadcast: Chris Benoit and William Regal overcame Ric Flair and Batista; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus pinned Victoria; Tyson Tomko downed Steven Richards; Chris Jericho bested Christian in a ladder match to win the vacant WWE Intercontinental Champion; Shawn Michaels toppled Kane in a no-DQ match; WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rob Conway overturned Rhyno and Tajiri; Triple H rolled over World Heavyweight Champion Randy Orton to win the title.

3rd October 2004: “WWE No Mercy 2004”: East Rutherford, NJ – 10,000
($700,000 live gate)
Heat: (10/03): Mark Jindrak beat Scotty 2 Hotty.
PPV Broadcast: Eddie Guerrero overcame Luther Reigns; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Spike Dudley beat Nunzio; Billy Kidman toppled Paul London; WWE Tag Team Champions Kenzo Suzuki and Rene Dupree downed Rey Mysterio and Rob Van Dam; The Big Show toppled Kurt Angle; WWE US Championship Best of 5 Series: John Cena bested WWE US Champion Booker T to win the title (Best of 5 series); Charlie Haas, Rico, and Jackie Gayda defeated The Dudley Boys and Dawn Marie; WWE World Champion John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over The Undertaker in a “Last Ride” match.

19th October 2004: “WWE Taboo Tuesday 2004”: Milwaukee, WI – 3,500
($215,000 live gate)
Dark match: Sgt Slaughter beat Muhammad Hassan by DQ.
PPV Broadcast: Shelton Benjamin bested WWE Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho to win the title; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus won a “school girl” battle royal; Snitsky overcame Kane in a chain match; Eugene overcame Eric Bischoff in a “hair vs hair” match; Chris Benoit and Edge dropped WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rob Conway to win the titles; Christy Hemme beat Carmella in a “lingerie pillow fight”; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Shawn Michaels; Randy Orton rolled over Ric Flair in a cage match.

14th November 2004: “Survivor Series 2004”: Cleveland, OH – 7,500
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (11/14): WWE World Tag Champions Rob Conway and Sylvain Grenier defeated The Hurricane and Rosey in a non-title match.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Cruiserweight Champion Spike Dudley beat Billy Kidman, Rey Mysterio, and Chavo Guerrero Jr in a four-way; WWE Intercontinental Champion Shelton Benjamin bested Christian; Eddie Guerrero, The Big Show, John Cena, and Rob Van Dam rolled over Kurt Angle, WWE US Champion Carlito, Mark Jindrak, and Luther Reigns in a “Survivor Series” elimination match; The Undertaker bested John Heidenreich; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus overcame Lita by DQ; WWE World Champion John Bradshaw Layfield pinned Booker T; Randy Orton, Chris Benoit, Maven, and Chris Jericho bested World Heavyweight Champion Triple H, Batista, Snitsky, and Edge in a “Survivor Series” elimination match.

12th December 2004: “WWE Armageddon 2004”: Atlanta, GA – 4,500
($300,000 live gate)
Heat: (12/12): Billy Kidman and Akio defeated Chavo Guerrero Jr and Paul London.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Team Champions Rob Van Dam and Rey Mysterio dropped Kenzo Suzuki and Rene Dupree; Kurt Angle toppled “Santa Claus” in a “Kurt Angle Invitational”; Daniel Puder beat The Miz in a boxing match; Doug and Danny Basham beat Charlie Haas and Hardcore Holly; WWE US Champion John Cena bested Jesus in a streetfight; Dawn Marie pinned Jackie Gayda. Charlie Haas was guest referee; The Big Show toppled Kurt Angle, Mark Jindrak, and Luther Reigns in a handicap match; Sho Funaki overturned WWE Cruiserweight Champion Spike Dudley to win the title; WWE World Champion John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over Eddie Guerrero, The Undertaker, and Booker T in a no-DQ four-way match.
