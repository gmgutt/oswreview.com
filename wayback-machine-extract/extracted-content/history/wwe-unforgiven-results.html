WWE Unforgiven Results

WWF/E UNFORGIVEN RESULTS

26th April 1998: “WWF IYH: Unforgiven”: Greensboro, NC – 21,427 sellout (20,268 paid)
($341,270 live gate)
($165,572 merchandise sales)
Ken Shamrock, Faarooq and Steve Blackman defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock, D-Lo Brown and Mark Henry; WWF European Champion Triple H dropped Owen Hart. Chyna was suspended above the ring in a cage; NWA Tag Team Champions Bob Holly and Bart Gunn overcame Ricky Morton and Robert Gibson; Luna Vachon overcame Sable in an “evening gown” match; WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn overturned The Legion of Doom; The Undertaker bested Kane in an “inferno” match; Dude Love rolled over WWF World Champion Steve Austin by DQ.

26th September 1999: “WWF Unforgiven 1999”: Charlotte, NC – 15,779
($505,477 live gate)
($93,006 merchandise sales)
Val Venis beat Steve Blackman; D-Lo Brown pinned WWF European Champion Mark Henry to win the title; WWF Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett downed Chyna by reverse decision-DQ; The Acolytes bested The Dudley Boys; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory defeated Luna in a hardcore match; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg toppled Edge and Christian; WWF Hardcore Champion Al Snow overcame The Big Bossman in a “Kennel from Hell” match; X-Pac toppled Chris  Jericho by DQ;  Triple H bested The Rock, Mankind, The Big Show, Davey Boy Smith and Kane in a ”six-pack” match to win the vacant WWF World Title.

24th September 2000: “WWF Unforgiven 2000”: Philadelphia, PA – 18,092 sellout (16,862 paid)
($822,400 live gate)
($172,200 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Steven Richards, Bull Buchanon, The Goodfather, and Val Venis overturned The Acolytes and The Dudley Boys; Tazz defeated Jerry Lawler in a strap match; WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman downed WWF European Champion Al Snow, Crash Holly, Test, Perry Saturn, and Sho Funaki in a 10-minute “hardcore challenge” match (Crash Holly pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman to win the title; Perry Saturn pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Steve Blackman pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Perry Saturn to win the title); Chris Jericho bested X-Pac; The Hardy Boys overturned WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian in a cage match; WWF Intercontinental Champion Eddie Guerrero dropped Rikishi by DQ; Triple H pinned Kurt Angle in a no-DQ match; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over The Undertaker, Kane, and Chris Benoit in a four-way.

23rd September 2001: “WWF Unforgiven 2001”: Pittsburgh, PA – 13,855 (12,887 paid)
($640,115 live gate)
($50,702 merchandise sales)
Heat: (09/23): Billy Gunn bested Tommy Dreamer.
PPV Broadcast: WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned The Big Show and Spike Dudley, Lance Storm and WWF European Champion The Hurricane, and The Hardy Boys in a four-way elimination match; Perry Saturn bested Raven; Christian downed WWF Intercontinental Champion Edge to win the title; WCW World Tag Champions The Undertaker and Kane toppled Kronik; WWF Hardcore Champion Rob Van Dam rolled over Chris Jericho; WCW World Champion The Rock defeated Booker T and Shane McMahon in a handicap match; Rhyno overturned WCW US Champion Tajiri to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled WWF World Champion Steve Austin to win the title.

22nd September 2002: “WWE Unforgiven 2002”: Los Angeles, CA – 16,000 sellout
Heat: (09/22): Rey Mysterio dropped Chavo Guerrero Jr.
PPV Broadcast: Booker T, Kane, Bubba Ray Dudley, and Goldust overcame Test, William Regal, and WWE World Tag Champions Lance Storm and Christian; WWE Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho bested Ric Flair; Eddie Guerrero overcame Edge; Rosey and Jamal beat Billy Gunn and Chuck Palumbo; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Rob Van Dam; Trish Stratus overcame WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly to win the title; Chris Benoit toppled Kurt Angle; The Undertaker battled WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar to a no-contest.

21st September 2003: “WWE Unforgiven 2003”: Hershey, PA – 10,347 
($560,000 live gate)
Heat: (09/21): Maven bested Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: The Dudley Boys overturned Rob Conway, and WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree in a handicap tables match to win the titles; Test beat Scott Steiner in a “Stacy’s services vs. Steiner’s services” encounter; Randy Orton bested Shawn Michaels; Lita and Trish Stratus overcame WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly and Gail Kim; Kane toppled Shane McMahon in a “last man standing” match; WWE Intercontinental Champion Christian downed Rob Van Dam and Chris Jericho in a three-way; Al Snow and Jonathan Coachman bested Jerry Lawler and Jim Ross in a “Winner announces Raw” match; Bill Goldberg pinned World Heavyweight Champion Triple H to win the title in a “Title vs. Career” encounter.

12th September 2004: “WWE Unforgiven 2004”: Portland, OR – 10,000
($675,000 live gate)
Heat: (09/12): Maven beat Rodney Mack.
PPV Broadcast: Chris Benoit and William Regal overcame Ric Flair and Batista; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus pinned Victoria; Tyson Tomko downed Steven Richards; Chris Jericho bested Christian in a ladder match to win the vacant WWE Intercontinental Champion; Shawn Michaels toppled Kane in a no-DQ match; WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rob Conway overturned Rhyno and Tajiri; Triple H rolled over World Heavyweight Champion Randy Orton to win the title.

18th September 2005: “WWE Unforgiven 2005”: Oklahoma City, OK – 8,000
($485,000 live gate)
Heat: Rob Conway beat Tajiri.
PPV Broadcast: Ric Flair defeated WWE Intercontinental Champion Carlito to win the title; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus and Ashley dropped Victoria and Torrie Wilson; The Big Show pinned Gene Snitsky; Shelton Benjamin downed Kerwin White; Matt Hardy toppled Edge in a cage match; Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch overturned WWE World Tag Champions The Hurricane and Rosey to win the titles; Shawn Michaels bested Chris Masters; Kurt Angle rolled over WWE World Champion John Cena by DQ.

17th September 2006: “WWE Unforgiven 2006”: Toronto, ONT – 16,105 sellout (15,000 paid)
($800,000 live gate)
Dark match: Super Crazy downed Shelton Benjamin.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Intercontinental Champion Johnny Nitro pinned Jeff Hardy; Kane battled Umaga to a double count-out; WWE World Tag Champions Mikey and Kenny  defeated Robbie and Rory McAllister; Shawn Michaels and Triple H toppled ECW World Champion The Big Show, Vince and Shane McMahon in a handicap “Hell in a Cell” match; Trish Stratus toppled WWE Women’s Champion Lita to win the title; Randy Orton beat Carlito; John Cena rolled over WWE World Champion Edge in a TLC encounter to win the title.

16th September 2007: “WWE Unforgiven 2007”: Memphis, TN – 12,000
($550,000 live gate)
Dark match: Kane bested Kenny Dykstra.
PPV Broadcast: ECW Champion CM Punk overturned Elijah Burke; WWE Tag Champions MVP and Matt Hardy downed Deuce and Domimo; Triple H pinned Carlito in a “no-DQ for Carlito” match; WWE Women’s Champion Candice Michelle beat Beth Phoenix; Batista rolled over World Heavyweight Champion The Great Khali and Rey Mysterio in a three-way to win the title; WWE World Tag Champions Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch overturned Paul London and Brian Kendrick; Randy Orton bested WWE World Champion John Cena by DQ; The Undertaker rolled over Mark Henry.

7th September 2008: “WWE Unforgiven 2008”: Cleveland, OH – 8,707 (61% full)
($531,880 live gate)
Dark match: Evan Bourne beat John Morrison.
PPV Broadcast: Matt Hardy won the ECW Title in a “scramble” match over Mark Henry, Chavo Guerrero, Finlay, and The Miz; WWE World Tag Champions Cody Rhodes and Ted Dibiase Jr overturned Cryme Tyme; Shawn Michaels bested Chris Jericho by stoppage in an “unsanctioned” match; WWE World Champion Triple H toppled Jeff Hardy, MVP, Brian Kendrick, and Shelton Benjamin in a “scramble” match; WWE Divas Champion Michelle McCool dropped Maryse; Chris Jericho won the World Heavyweight Title over Batista, Kane, Rey Mysterio, and JBL.
