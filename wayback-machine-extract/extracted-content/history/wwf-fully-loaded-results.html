WWF FULLY LOADED RESULTS

26th July 1998: “WWF IYH: Fully Loaded”: Fresno, CA – 9,855 sellout (8,835 paid)
($179,435 live gate)
($71,239 merchandise sales)
Val Venis beat Jeff Jarrett; WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown defeated X-Pac; Scorpio and Faarooq downed Terry Funk and Bradshaw; Mark Henry pinned Vader; Skull and Eight-Ball overcame The Legion of Doom; Owen Hart overturned Ken Shamrock in a “dungeon” match. Dan Severn was guest referee; Triple H battled WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock to a draw in a “best-of-three-falls” outing; Sable defeated Jackie in a bikini contest; WWF World Champion Steve Austin and The Undertaker bested WWF World Tag Champions Kane and Mankind to win the titles.

25th July 1999: “WWF Fully Loaded 1999”: Buffalo, NY – 16,605 (15,194 paid)
($547,380 live gate)
($102,007 merchandise sales)
Jeff Jarrett defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion Edge to win the title; The Acolytes beat Michael Hayes and WWF World Tag Champions Matt and Jeff Hardy in a no-DQ, handicap match to win the titles; D-Lo Brown pinned WWF European Champion Mideon to win the title; The Big Bossman bested WWF Hardcore Champion Al Snow to win the title; The Big Show downed Kane. Hardcore Holly was guest referee; Ken Shamrock dropped Steve Blackman in an Iron Circle match; X-Pac and Road Dogg toppled Billy Gunn and Chyna; Triple H bested The Rock in a no-DQ, falls-count-anywhere, strap match; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker in a First Blood match.

23rd July 2000: “WWF Fully Loaded 2000”: Dallas, TX – 16,504 (14,668 paid)
($788,410 live gate)
($157,048 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Lita and The Hardy Boys overturned Test, Albert, and Trish Stratus; Tazz defeated Al Snow; Perry Saturn pinned WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero to win the title; The Acolytes overturned WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Val Venis overturned Rikishi in a cage match; The Undertaker beat Kurt Angle; Triple H bested Chris Jericho in a “Last Man Standing” match; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over Chris Benoit.
