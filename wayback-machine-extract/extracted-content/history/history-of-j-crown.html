History of J-Crown

HISTORY OF THE J-CROWN

See also: History of IWGP Jr Title

NB: The J-Crown was a temporary unification of 8 different championship belts: the IWGP Jr Title (NJPW), the WAR IJ Heavyweight Title (WAR), the British Commonwealth Jr Champion (Michinoku Pro), the NWA Jr Title (Wrestle Dream), the NWA World Welterweight Title (CMLL), the UWA Jr Title (UWA), the WWA Jr Title (WWA) and the WWF Light Heavyweight Title (WWF)

DATE WRESTLER DAYS HELD LOCATION SUCCESSFUL DEFENCES
08/05/1996 The Great Sasuke 67 Tokyo, Japan 0
The J-Crown is created after an 8-man tournament of champions. IWGP Jr, NWA Jr, WWF Light Heavyweight, & WWA Jr Light Heavyweight Champion The Great Sasuke defeated WAR IJ, British Jr, UWA Jr & NWA Welterweight Champion Ultimo Dragon in the tournament final to unify all the titles into the J-Crown.
10/11/1996 Último Dragón 85 Osaka, Japan 7
01/04/1997 Jushin Liger 183 Tokyo, Japan 4
Liger drops the WAR IJ Heavyweight Title to Yuji Yasuraoka on 06/06/1997 on a WAR show in Tokyo. The title is removed from the J-Crown. From this point forward the J-Crown represents only 7 titles.
07/06/1997 El Samurai  35 Sapporo, Japan 0
08/10/1997 Shinjiro Otani 87 Nagoya, Japan 3
11/05/1997 TITLE DISBANDED
After the WWF demands the WWF Light Heavyweight Title be removed from the J-Crown and returned to them, Otani elects to vacate all the titles apart from the IWGP Jr Title. The J-Crown is disbanded from this date.
