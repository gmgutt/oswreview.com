WWF PPV Results 2000

WWF PPV RESULTS (2000)

See also: WWF PPV Statistics 2000

23rd January 2000: “WWF Royal Rumble 2000”: New York, NY (MSG) – 19,231 sellout (16,629 paid)
($1,142,540 live gate)
($157,375 merchandise sales)
Set record for the largest MSG gate in history at that point in time.
Tazz bested Kurt Angle; Matt and Jeff Hardy toppled The Dudley Boys in a tables match; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho defeated Chyna and Hardcore Holly in a Triple Threat to become the sole holder of the title; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg downed The Acolytes; WWF World Champion Triple H rolled over Cactus Jack in a streetfight; The Rock won the 30-man Royal Rumble by last eliminating The Big Show.

27th February 2000: “WWF No Way Out 2000”: Hartford, CT – 12,551 sellout
($451,625 live gate)
($97,126 merchandise sales)
WWF European Champion Kurt Angle pinned WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho to win the title; The Dudley Boys overcame WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg to win the titles; Mark Henry beat Viscera; Edge and Christian downed Matt and Jeff Hardy; Tazz toppled The Big Bossman by DQ; X-Pac bested Kane in a no-holds-barred match; Rikishi and Too Cool overturned Chris Benoit, Dean Malenko and Perry Saturn; The Big Show toppled The Rock; WWF World Champion Triple H  rolled over Cactus Jack in a “Title vs. Career” Hell-in-a-Cell match.

2nd April 2000: “WWF Wrestlemania 2000”: Anaheim, CA – 18,034 (16,716 paid)
($1,347,800 live gate)
($277,125 merchandise sales)
The Big Bossman and Bull Buchanan defeated D-Lo Brown and The Godfather;
*** Hardcore Battle Royal: Tazz pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Viscera pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Tazz to win the title; Sho Funaki pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Viscera to win the title; Rodney Gas pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Sho Funaki to win the title; Joey Abs pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Rodney Gas to win the title; Thrasher pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Joey Abs to win the title; Pete Gas pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Thrasher to win the title; Tazz pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Pete Gas to win the title; Crash Holly pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Tazz to win the title; Hardcore Holly pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly to win the title;
Test and Albert downed Al Snow and Steve Blackman; Edge and Christian overcame WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys, and Matt and Jeff Hardy in a Triple Threat ladder match to win the titles; Terri beat The Kat. Val Venis was guest referee; Too Cool and Chyna overturned Eddie Guerrero, Perry Saturn and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko;
*** Two-Fall Triple Threat encounter for the WWF Intercontinental Title and WWF European Title: Kurt Angle vs. Chris Jericho vs. Chris Benoit: Chris Benoit pinned Chris Jericho to win the WWF Intercontinental Title; Chris Jericho pinned Chris Benoit to lift the WWF Intercontinental Title;
Kane and Rikishi bested Road Dogg and X-Pac; WWF World Champion Triple H rolled over The Rock, Mick Foley and The Big Show in a Fatal Four Way elimination match.

30th April 2000: “WWF Backlash 2000”: Washington, DC – 17,867 sellout (16,614 paid)
($540,820 live gate)
($170,693 merchandise sales)
WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian bested X-Pac and Road Dogg; WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko pinned Scotty 2 Hotty; The Big Bossman and Bull Buchanon defeated The Acolytes; WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly beat Matt Hardy, Jeff Hardy, Tazz, Perry Saturn and Hardcore Holly in a six-way hardcore match; The Big Show squashed Kurt Angle; Test and Albert dropped The Dudley Boys; WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero toppled Essa Rios; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Benoit bested Chris Jericho by DQ; The Rock rolled over WWF World Champion Triple H to win the title. Shane McMahon was guest referee.

21st May 2000: “WWE Judgment Day 2000”: Louisville, KY – 16,827 sellout (15,886 paid)
($596,050 live gate)
($130,256 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Rikishi and Too Cool bested Kurt Angle and WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian; WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero dropped Perry Saturn and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko in a three-way;  Shane McMahon downed The Big Show in a no-DQ match; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Benoit overcame Chris Jericho in a submission match; X-Pac and Road Dogg defeated The Dudley Boys in a tables match; Triple H bested WWF World Champion The Rock in a 60-minute Ironman match to win the title. 6:5.

25th June 2000: “WWF King of the Ring 2000”: Boston, MA – 17,651 sellout (14,464 paid)
($865,778 live gate)
PPV Broadcast: WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi toppled Chris Benoit by DQ (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Val Venis overturned WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero in a non-title match (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Crash Holly overcame Bull Buchanon (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Kurt Angle toppled Chris Jericho (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Edge and Christian downed WWF World Tag Champions Scotty 2 Hotty and Brian Christopher, The Hardy Boys, and Test and Albert in a four-way elimination match to win the titles; WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi toppled Val Venis (KOTR 2000 – Semi-Final); Kurt Angle toppled Crash Holly (KOTR 2000 – Semi-Final); WWF Hardcore Champion Pat Patterson battled Gerald Brisco to a no-contest in an “Evening Gown” match (Crash Holly overcame WWF Hardcore Champion Pat Patterson to win the title); X-Pac and Road Dogg defeated The Dudley Boys in a handicap “dumpster” match; Kurt Angle toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi in a non-title match (KOTR 2000 – Final); The Rock, The Undertaker, and Kane toppled WWF World Champion Triple H, Vince and Shane McMahon. By virtue of the win, Rock was awarded the WWF World Title.

23rd July 2000: “WWF Fully Loaded 2000”: Dallas, TX – 16,504 (14,668 paid)
($788,410 live gate)
($157,048 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Lita and The Hardy Boys overturned Test, Albert, and Trish Stratus; Tazz defeated Al Snow; Perry Saturn pinned WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero to win the title; The Acolytes overturned WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Val Venis overturned Rikishi in a cage match; The Undertaker beat Kurt Angle; Triple H bested Chris Jericho in a “Last Man Standing” match; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over Chris Benoit.

27th August 2000: “WWF Summerslam 2000”: Raleigh, NC – 17,002 sellout (15,603 paid)
($1,151,940 live gate)
($147,815 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Steven Richards, Bull Buchanon, and The Goodfather beat Rikishi, Brian Christopher, and Scotty 2 Hotty; X-Pac pinned Road Dogg; Eddie Guerrero and Chyna defeated WWF Intercontinental Champion Val Venis and Trish Stratus. By virtue of a pre-match stipulation, Chyna was awarded the WWF Intercontinental Title; Jerry Lawler beat Tazz; Steve Blackman downed WWF Hardcore Champion Shane McMahon to win the title; Chris Benoit overcame Chris Jericho in a “best-of-three-falls” match. 2:1; WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian downed The Dudley Boys and The Hardy Boys in a three-way TLC match; The Kat overcame Terri in a “Stink Face” match; The Undertaker battled Kane to a no-contest; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over Kurt Angle and Triple H in a three-way.

24th September 2000: “WWF Unforgiven 2000”: Philadelphia, PA – 18,092 sellout (16,862 paid)
($822,400 live gate)
($172,200 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Steven Richards, Bull Buchanon, The Goodfather, and Val Venis overturned The Acolytes and The Dudley Boys; Tazz defeated Jerry Lawler in a strap match; WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman downed WWF European Champion Al Snow, Crash Holly, Test, Perry Saturn, and Sho Funaki in a 10-minute “hardcore challenge” match (Crash Holly pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman to win the title; Perry Saturn pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Steve Blackman pinned WWF Hardcore Champion Perry Saturn to win the title); Chris Jericho bested X-Pac; The Hardy Boys overturned WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian in a cage match; WWF Intercontinental Champion Eddie Guerrero dropped Rikishi by DQ; Triple H pinned Kurt Angle in a no-DQ match; WWF World Champion The Rock rolled over The Undertaker, Kane, and Chris Benoit in a four-way.

22nd October 2000: “WWF No Mercy 2000”: Albany, NY – 14,342 sellout (13,872 paid)
($693,225 live gate)
($98,671 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: The Dudley Boys overturned Bull Buchanon and The Goodfather, Too Cool, D-Lo Brown and Chaz, and Raven and Tazz in a “tag team table invitational” gauntlet match; The Acolytes and WWF Women’s Champion Lita battled Test, Albert, and Trish Stratus to a no-contest; Chris Jericho dropped X-Pac in a cage match; Val Venis and Stevie Richards overturned Billy Gunn and Chyna; Steve Austin battled Rikishi to a no-contest in a “No Holds Barred” match; WWF European Champion William Regal bested Mideon; Los Conquistadors (Edge and Christian under masks) defeated WWF World Tag Champions The Hardy Boys to win the titles; Triple H pinned Chris Benoit; Kurt Angle toppled WWF World Champion The Rock in a no-DQ match to win the title.

19th November 2000: “WWF Survivor Series 2000”: Tampa, FL – 16,533 sellout (14,753 paid)
($778,990 live gate)
($114,352 merchandise sales)
Heat: (11/19): Val Venis beat Jeff Hardy
PPV Broadcast: WWF Hardcore Champion Steve Blackman, Crash and Molly Holly overcame Test, Albert, and Trish Stratus; Chris Benoit, WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko, WWF Intercontinental Champion Eddie Guerrero, and Perry Saturn defeated Billy Gunn, Road Dogg, Chyna, and K-Kwik in a “Survivor Series” elimination match; Kane toppled Chris Jericho; WWF European Champion William Regal bested Hardcore Holly by DQ; The Rock rolled over Rikishi; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory overcame Lita; WWF World Champion Kurt Angle toppled The Undertaker; The Hardy Boys and The Dudley Boys overturned Edge, Christian, WWF World Tag Champions Bull Buchanon and The Goodfather in a “Survivor Series” elimination match; Steve Austin battled Triple H to a no-contest in a no-DQ match.

10th December 2000: “WWF Armageddon 2000”: Birmingham, AL – 14,920 (11,924 paid)
($573,990 live gate)
($83,977 merchandise sales)
Heat: (12/10): Scotty 2 Hotty beat D-Lo Brown.
PPV Broadcast: WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko, Eddie Guerrero, and Perry Saturn defeated Lita and The Hardy Boys in an elimination match; WWF European Champion William Regal bested Hardcore Holly; Val Venis pinned Chyna; Chris Jericho dropped Kane in a “Last Man Standing” match; Edge and Christian downed WWF World Tag Champions The Goodfather and Bull Buchanon, Road Dogg and K-Kwik, and The Dudley Boys in a four-way to win the titles; Chris Benoit overcame WWF Intercontinental Champion Billy Gunn to win the title; WWF Women’s Champion Ivory overcame Trish Stratus and Molly Holly in a three-way; WWF World Champion Kurt Angle toppled Steve Austin, Rikishi, The Undertaker, The Rock, and Triple H in a six-way “Hell in a Cell” match.
