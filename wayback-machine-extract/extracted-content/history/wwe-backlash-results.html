WWE Backlash Results

WWF/E BACKLASH RESULTS

25th April 1999: “WWF Backlash 1999”: Providence, RI – 10,939 sellout
($293,873 live gate)
($83,282 merchandise sales)
The Acolytes and Mideon defeated Edge, Christian and Gangrel; Al Snow downed WWF Hardcore Champion Hardcore Holly to win the title; WWF Intercontinental Champion The Godfather bested Goldust; Road Dogg and Billy Gunn toppled Owen Hart and Jeff Jarrett; Mankind dropped The Big Show in a Boiler Room Brawl; Triple H pinned X-Pac; The Undertaker overturned Ken Shamrock; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Rock in a “No Holds Barred” match. Shane McMahon was guest referee.

30th April 2000: “WWF Backlash 2000”: Washington, DC – 17,867 sellout (16,614 paid)
($540,820 live gate)
($170,693 merchandise sales)
WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian bested X-Pac and Road Dogg; WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko pinned Scotty 2 Hotty; The Big Bossman and Bull Buchanon defeated The Acolytes; WWF Hardcore Champion Crash Holly beat Matt Hardy, Jeff Hardy, Tazz, Perry Saturn and Hardcore Holly in a six-way hardcore match; The Big Show squashed Kurt Angle; Test and Albert dropped The Dudley Boys; WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero toppled Essa Rios; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Benoit bested Chris Jericho by DQ; The Rock rolled over WWF World Champion Triple H to win the title. Shane McMahon was guest referee.

29th April 2001: “WWE Backlash 2001”: Chicago, IL – 15,592 sellout (14,751 paid)
($831,510 live gate)
($143,908 merchandise sales)
Heat: (04/29): Jerry Lynn bested WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Crash Holly to win the title; Lita beat Molly Holly.
PPV Broadcast: X-Pac, Justin Credible, and Albert overcame Bubba Ray, D-Von, and Spike Dudley; WWF Hardcore Champion Rhyno overturned Raven; William Regal pinned Chris Jericho in a “Dutchess of Queensbury Rules” match; Chris Benoit toppled Kurt Angle in a 30-minute “Ultimate Submission” match. 4:3; Shane McMahon bested The Big Show in a “Last Man Standing” match; WWF European Champion Matt Hardy won a three-way over Christian and Eddie Guerrero; WWF World Champion Steve Austin and WWF Intercontinental Champion Triple H rolled over WWF World Tag Champions The Undertaker and Kane to win the titles.

21st April 2002: “WWF Backlash 2002”: Kansas City, MO – 12,489
Heat: (04/21): The Big Show toppled Justin Credible and Steven Richards in a handicap match.
PPV Broadcast: Tajiri beat WWF Cruiserweight Champion Billy Kidman to win the title; Scott Hall defeated Bradshaw; WWF Women’s Champion Jazz defeated Trish Stratus; Brock Lesnar downed Jeff Hardy; Kurt Angle toppled Edge; Eddie Guerrero overcame WWF Intercontinental Champion Rob Van Dam to win the title; The Undertaker bested Steve Austin. Ric Flair was guest referee; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Chuck Palumbo dropped Al Snow and Maven; Hulk Hogan bested WWF World Champion Triple H to win the title.

27th April 2003: “WWE Backlash 2003”: Worcester, MA – 10,000
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (04/27): Scott Steiner dropped Rico.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Team Champions Charlie Haas and Shelton Benjamin bested Eddie and Chavo Guerrero Jr; Sean O’Haire beat Rikishi; WWE World Tag Champions Rob Van Dam and Kane toppled The Dudley Boys. Chief Morley was guest referee; Jazz overturned WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus to win the title; The Big Show toppled Rey Mysterio; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped John Cena; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H, Ric Flair, and Chris Jericho bested Kevin Nash, Shawn Michaels, and Booker T; Bill Goldberg pinned The Rock.

18th April 2004: “WWE Backlash 2004”: Edmonton, Alberta – 13,000
($483,000 live gate)
Heat: (04/18): Val Venis overturned Matt Hardy.
PPV Broadcast: Shelton Benjamin bested Ric Flair; Jonathan Coachman defeated Tajiri; Chris Jericho toppled Christian and Trish Stratus in a handicap match; WWE Women’s Champion Victoria beat Lita; WWE Intercontinental Champion Randy Orton rolled over Mick Foley in a “falls count anywhere, no holds barred” match; The Hurricane and Rosey beat Rob Conway and Sylvain Grenier; Edge toppled Kane; World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit won a three-way over Triple H and Shawn Michaels.

1st May 2005: “WWE Backlash 2005”: Manchester, NH – 14,000 sellout (12,000 paid)
($700,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/01): Tyson Tomko beat Val Venis.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Intercontinental Champion Shelton Benjamin defeated Chris Jericho; The Hurricane and Rosey downed WWE World Tag Champions William Regal and Tajiri, The Heart Throbs, Rob Conway and Sylvan Grenier, and Maven and Simon Dean in a “tag team turmoil” match to win the titles; Edge dropped Chris Benoit in a “last man standing” match; Kane pinned Viscera; Shawn Michaels and Hulk Hogan bested Muhammad Hassan and Daivari; World Heavyweight Champion Batista downed Triple H.

30th April 2006: “WWE Backlash 2006”: Lexington, KY – 14,000
($480,000 live gate)
Heat: (04/30): Goldust dropped Rob Conway.
PPV Broadcast: Carlito pinned Chris Masters; Umaga toppled Ric Flair; Trish Stratus toppled WWE Women’s Champion Mickie James by DQ; Rob Van Dam downed WWE Intercontinental Champion Shelton Benjamin to win the title; The Big Show battled Kane to a no-contest; Vince McMahon and Shane McMahon beat Shawn Michaels in a “no holds barred” handicap match; WWE World Champion John Cena rolled over Triple H and Edge in a no-DQ three-way.

29th April 2007: “WWE Backlash 2007”: Atlanta, GA – 14,000 sellout (13,000 paid)
($650,000 live gate)
Dark match: Carlito beat Johnny Nitro.
PPV Broadcast: WWE World Tag Champions Matt and Jeff Hardy downed Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch; WWE Women’s Champion Melina pinned Mickie James; WWE US Champion Chris Benoit dropped MVP; Umaga, Vince McMahon and Shane McMahon defeated ECW Champion Bobby Lashley in a handicap match. By virtue of a pre-match stipulation, Vince won the ECW Title; World Heavyweight Champion The Undertaker battled Batista to a draw in a “last man standing” match; WWE World Champion John Cena rolled over Randy Orton, Shawn Michaels, and Edge in a four-way.

27th April 2008: “WWE Backlash 2008”: Baltimore, MD –11,277 sellout (9,000 paid)
($624,195 live gate)
Dark match: WWE Tag Champions John Morrison and The Miz overcame Jimmy Wang Yang and Shannon Moore.
PPV Broadcast: Matt Hardy overcame WWE US Champion MVP to win the title; ECW Champion Kane dropped Chavo Guerrero Jr; The Big Show pinned The Great Khali; Shawn Michaels toppled Batista. Chris Jericho was guest referee; Beth Phoenix, Jillian Hall, Melina, Layla, Victoria, and Natalya Neidhart overturned WWE Women’s Champion Mickie James, Michelle McCool, Kelly Kelly, Ashley, and Cherry; World Heavyweight Champion The Undertaker rolled over Edge; Triple H bested WWE World Champion Randy Orton, John Cena, and John Bradshaw Layfield in a no-DQ, no count-out, four-way elimination match to win the title.

26th April 2009: “WWE Backlash 2009”: Providence, RI – 8,357 sellout
($523,855 live gate)
Dark match: Kofi Kingston beat Dolph Ziggler.
PPV Broadcast: Christian defeated ECW Champion Jack Swagger to win the title; Chris Jericho downed Ricky Steamboat; Kane pinned CM Punk; Jeff Hardy bested Matt Hardy in an I Quit match; Santina Marella beat Beth Phoenix; Randy Orton, Ted Dibiase Jr, and Cody Rhodes overturned WWE World Champion Triple H, Batista, and Shane McMahon. By virtue of a stipulation, Orton won the WWE World Title; Edge rolled over World Heavyweight Champion John Cena in a “last man standing” match to win the title.

11th September 2016: “WWE Backlash 2016”: Richmond, VA – 7,000
Shown live on PPV/WWE Network
Pre-show: Baron Corbin overcame Apollo Crews.
PPV Broadcast: Becky Lynch defeated Alexa Bliss, Carmella, Naomi, Natalya and Nikki Bella in a six-way to become the first WWE Smackdown Women’s Champion; Jimmy Uso and Jey Uso bested Zack Ryder and Mojo Rawley (WWE Smackdown Tag Title Tournament – Second Chance Semi-Final); WWE Intercontinental Champion The Miz defeated Dolph Ziggler; Kane downed Bray Wyatt in a “No Holds Barred” match; Heath Slater and Rhyno bested Jimmy Uso and Jey Uso to become the first WWE Smackdown Tag Champions (WWE Smackdown Tag Title Tournament – Final); AJ Styles overturned WWE World Champion Dean Ambrose to win the title.
