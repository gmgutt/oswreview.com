WWE Judgment Day Results

WWF/E JUDGMENT DAY RESULTS

18th October 1998: “WWF IYH: Judgment Day”: Chicago, IL – 18,153 sellout (16,458 paid)
($357,050 live gate)
($144,930 merchandise sales)
Heat: (10/18): Steve Blackman beat Bradshaw; Giant Silva, Kurrgan and Golga downed Jose Estrada, Miguel Perez and Jesus Castillo; The Godfather defeated Faarooq; Scorpio dropped Jeff Jarrett.
PPV Broadcast: Al Snow beat Marc Mero; The Legion of Doom and Droz defeated Skull, Eight-Ball and Paul Ellering; Christian pinned WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku to win the title; Goldust bested Val Venis; X-Pac overcame WWF European Champion D-Lo Brown to win the title; The Headbangers overturned WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg by DQ; WWF Intercontinental Champion Ken Shamrock toppled Mankind; Mark Henry rolled over The Rock; The Undertaker battled Kane to a no-contest. Steve Austin was guest referee.

21st May 2000: “WWE Judgment Day 2000”: Louisville, KY – 16,827 sellout (15,886 paid)
($596,050 live gate)
($130,256 merchandise sales)
PPV Broadcast: Rikishi and Too Cool bested Kurt Angle and WWF World Tag Champions Edge and Christian; WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero dropped Perry Saturn and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Dean Malenko in a three-way;  Shane McMahon downed The Big Show in a no-DQ match; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Benoit overcame Chris Jericho in a submission match; X-Pac and Road Dogg defeated The Dudley Boys in a tables match; Triple H bested WWF World Champion The Rock in a 60-minute Ironman match to win the title. 6:5.

20th May 2001: “WWF Judgment Day 2001”:Sacramento, CA – 13,623 sellout
($674,855 live gate)
Heat: (05/20): Raven bested Val Venis; Hardcore and Crash Holly downed Taka Michinoku and Sho Funaki.
PPV Broadcast: William Regal pinned Rikishi; Kurt Angle toppled Chris Benoit in a “Three Stages of Hell” encounter; WWF Hardcore Champion Rhyno overturned The Big Show and Test in a three-way; WWF Women’s Champion Chyna pinned Lita; Kane toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion and WWF Tag Team Champion Triple H in a chain match; Chris Jericho and Chris Benoit won a “Tag Team Turmoil” match over beat Edge and Christian, Justin Credible and X-Pac, The Hardy Boys, The Acolytes, The Dudley Boys, and Dean Malenko and Perry Saturn; WWF World Champion Steve Austin rolled over The Undertaker in a “no holds barred” match.

19th May 2002: “WWE Judgment Day 2002”: Nashville, TN – 14,500
Heat: (05/19): WWE European Champion William Regal overcame D-Lo Brown.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Intercontinental Champion Eddie Guerrero overcame Rob Van Dam; WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus beat Stacy Keibler; Brock Lesnar and Paul Heyman bested The Hardy Boys; Steve Austin bested Ric Flair and The Big Show in a handicap match; Edge toppled Kurt Angle in a “hair vs. hair” match; Triple H rolled over Chris Jericho in a “Hell in a Cell” match; Rikishi and Rico overcame WWE Tag Team Champions Billy Gunn and Chuck Palumbo to win the titles; The Undertaker rolled over WWE World Champion Hulk Hogan to win the title.

18th May 2003: “WWE Judgment Day 2003”: Charlotte, NC – 13,000
($650,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/18): The Hurricane dropped Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: John Cena, Chuck Palumbo, and Johnny Stamboli beat Chris Benoit, Rhyno, and Brian Kendrick; Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree downed Scott Steiner and Test; Eddie Guerrero and Tajiri dropped WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas in a ladder match to win the titles; Christian won a battle royal to win the vacant WWE Intercontinental Title; Torrie Wilson bested Sable in a bikini challenge; Mr America (a masked Hulk Hogan) pinned Roddy Piper; Kevin Nash defeated World Heavyweight Champion Triple H by DQ; WWE Women’s Champion Jazz beat Trish Stratus, Victoria, and Jacquelyn in a four-way; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped The Big Show in a stretcher match.

16th May 2004: “WWE Judgment Day 2004”: Los Angeles, CA – 18,722 sellout  (13,500 paid)
($813,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/16):  Mark Jindrak defeated Sho Funaki.
PPV Broadcast: Rey Mysterio and Rob Van Dam rolled over The Dudley Boys; Torrie Wilson bested Dawn Marie; Mordecai pinned Scotty 2 Hotty; WWE Tag Team Champions Charlie Haas and Rico overcame Hardcore Holly and Billy Gunn; Chavo Guerrero Jr beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Jacquelyn to win the title; WWE US Champion John Cena bested Rene Dupree; The Undertaker pinned Booker T; John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over WWE World Champion Eddie Guerrero by DQ.

22nd May 2005: “WWE Judgment Day 2005”: Minneapolis, MN – 9,500
($500,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/22): Nunzio beat Akio.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Champions Johnny Nitro and Joey Mercury defeated Hardcore Holly and Charlie Haas; Carlito downed The Big Show; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Paul London dropped Chavo Guerrero Jr; Booker T bested Kurt Angle; WWE US Champion Orlando Jordan toppled John Heidenreich; Rey Mysterio overturned Eddie Guerrero by DQ; WWE World Champion John Cena rolled over John Bradshaw Layfield in an “I Quit” match.

21st May 2006: “WWE Judgment Day 2006”: Phoenix, AZ – 14,000 sellout (9,800 paid)
($560,000 live gate)
Dark match: Matt Hardy downed Simon Dean
PPV Broadcast: Brian Kendrick and Paul London beat WWE Tag Champions Johnny Nitro and Joey Mercury to win the titles; Chris Benoit toppled Finlay; Jillian Hall overturned Melina; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Gregory Helms downed Super Crazy; Mark Henry downed Kurt Angle by count-out; Booker T pinned Bobby Lashley (KOTR 2006 – Final); The Great Khali overturned The Undertaker; World Heavyweight Champion Rey Mysterio bested WWE US Champion John Bradshaw Layfield.

20th May 2007: “WWE Judgment Day 2007”: St Louis, MO – 10,500 (9,399 paid)
($575,000 live gate)
Dark match: Kane bested William Regal.
PPV Broadcast: Ric Flair bested Carlito; Bobby Lashley toppled ECW Champion Vince McMahon, Shane McMahon, and Umaga in a handicap match; CM Punk overturned Elijah Burke; Randy Orton dropped Shawn Michaels by stoppage; WWE World Tag Champions Matt and Jeff Hardy downed Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch; World Heavyweight Champion Edge pinned Batista; MVP defeated WWE US Champion Chris Benoit in a “best of three falls” match to win the title;  WWE World Champion John Cena rolled over The Great Khali.

18th May 2008: “WWE Judgment Day 2008”: Omaha, NE – 11,324 (10,663 paid) (83% full)
($653,740 live gate)
Dark match: WWE World Tag Champions Hardcore Holly and Cody Rhodes downed Carlito and Santino Marella.
PPV Broadcast: John Cena dropped John Bradshaw Layfield; WWE Tag Champions John Morrison and The Miz overcame ECW Champion Kane and CM Punk; Shawn Michaels toppled WWE Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho in a non-title match; WWE Women’s Champion Mickie James defeated Melina and Beth Phoenix in a three-way; The Undertaker bested Edge by count-out; Jeff Hardy pinned MVP; WWE World Champion Triple H rolled over Randy Orton in a cage match.

17th May 2009 “WWE Judgment Day 2009”: Chicago, IL – 13,433 sellout (11,500 paid)
($852,985 live gate)
Dark match: Mickie James beat Beth Phoenix.
PPV Broadcast: Umaga defeated CM Punk; ECW Champion Christian downed Jack Swagger; John Morrison dropped Shelton Benjamin; WWE Intercontinental Champion Rey Mysterio pinned Chris Jericho; Batista bested WWE World Champion Randy Orton by DQ; John Cena toppled The Big Show; World Heavyweight Champion Edge rolled over Jeff Hardy.
