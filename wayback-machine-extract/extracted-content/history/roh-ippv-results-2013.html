ROH iPPV Results 2013

ROH iPPV RESULTS 2013

2nd March 2013: “ROH 11th Anniversary Show”: Chicago Ridge, IL – 700
(1,000 iPPV Orders)
Dark match: Grizzly Redwood beat Darren Dean.
iPPV Broadcast: ACH won a six way over Tadarius Thomas, QT Marshall, Adam Page, Silas Young and Mike Sydal; Jimmy Jacobs and Steve Corino defeated Caprice Coleman and Cedric Alexander; BJ Whitmer bested Charlie Haas by stoppage in a “No Holds Barred” match; Davey Richards and Eddie Edwards toppled Rocky Romero and Alex Koslov; Michael Elgin dropped Roderick Strong in a “best-of-three-falls” match; Matt Tavern overcame ROH TV Champion Adam Cole to win the title; Bobby Fish and Kyle O’Reilly overturned ROH Tag Champions Mark and Jay Briscoe to win the titles; ROH Champion Kevin Steen rolled over Jay Lethal.

5th April 2013: “ROH Supercard of Honor VII”: Manhattan, NY (Hammerstein Ballrom) – 1,800 sellout
Set an all- time live gate record for the promotion.
Shown on iPPV
Dark match: Kongo beat Corey Hollis.
iPPV Broadcast: ACH and Tadarius Thomas beat QT Marshall and RD Evans; Mike Bennett defeated Shelton Benjamin; Michael Elgin downed Jay Lethal; Jimmy Rave, Jimmy Jacobs, Rhett Titus, Rhino and Cliff Compton dropped Mike Mondo, Caprice Coleman, Cedric Alexander, Mark Briscoe and BJ Whitmer; Karl Anderson bested Roderick Strong; ROH TV Champion Matt Tavern overturned Matt Hardy and Adam Cole in a three-way; ROH Tag Champions Kyle O’Reilly and Bobby Fish toppled Davey Richards and Eddie Edwards; Jay Briscoe rolled over ROH Champion Kevin Steen to win the title.

4th May 2013: “ROH Border Wars 2013”: Toronto, ONT – 1,100
Shown on iPPV
Dark match: Ethan Page and Josh Alexander beat Asylum and Matt Burns.
iPPV Broadcast: Cedric Alexander and Caprice Coleman beat ACH and Tadarius Thomas; Roderick Strong pinned Mike Bennett; BJ Whitmer defeated Rhett Titus in an “I Quit” match; Jimmy Jacobs and Cliff Compton downed Jay Lethal and Michael Elgin; Eddie Edwards dropped Taiji Ishimori; ROH TV Champion Matt Taven overcome Mark Briscoe; Davey Richards bested Paul London; ROH Champion Jay Briscoe rolled over Adam Cole.

22nd June 2013: “ROH Best in the World 2013”: Baltimore, MD – 800
Card was shown on iPPV
BJ Whitmer beat Mike Bennett; Davey Richards and Eddie Edwards defeated ACH and Tadarius Thomas; Adam Cole downed Roderick Strong by count-out; Michael Elgin dropped Tommaso Ciampa; ROH TV Champion Matt Tavern overturned Jay Lethal and Jimmy Jacobs in a three-way; ROH Tag Champions Bobby Fish and Kyle O’Reilly bested Caprice Coleman and Cedric Alexander, and Cliff Compton and Rhett Titus in a three-way; Matt Hardy toppled Kevin Steen in a no-DQ match; ROH Champion Jay Briscoe rolled over Mark Briscoe.

20th September 2013: “ROH Death Before Dishonor XI”: Philadelphia, PA – 550
Broadcast on Go Fight Live.
Was originally scheduled to be a VOD iPPV, but a few hours before the show, it was announced that the show could be watched free of charge.
Jay Lethal beat Silas Young; Adam Cole defeated Tommaso Ciampa (ROH Title Tournament – Semi-Final); Michael Elgin downed Kevin Steen (ROH Title Tournament – Semi-Final); IWGP Jr Tag Champions Rocky Romero and Alex Koslov dropped Eddie Edwards and Davey Richards; Adam Page overcame RC Evans; Roderick Strong bested Ricky Marvin; ACH, Tadarius Thomas, Cedric Alexander and Caprice Coleman toppled ROH Tag Champions Bobby Fish and Kyle O’Reilly, ROH TV Champion Matt Taven, and Michael Bennett; Adam Cole rolled over Michael Elgin to win the vacant ROH Title (ROH Title Tournament – Final).

14th December 2013: “ROH Final Battle 2013”: New York, NY (Hammerstein Ballroom) – 1,350
Shown on iPPV on 12/15/2013
Dark match: Bob Evans and Cheeseburger beat Bill Daly and Will Ferrara
iPPV Broadcast: Matt Hardy beat Adam Page; Silas Young defeated Mark Briscoe in a strap match; IWGP Jr Tag Champions The Young Bucks downed ACH and Tadarius Thomas; Kevin Steen dropped Michael Bennett in a stretcher match; ROH Tag Champions Bobby Fish and Kyle O’Reilly toppled Homicide and Eddie Kingston; Tommaso Ciampa overcame ROH TV Champion Matt Taven to win the title; Eddie Edwards and BJ Whitmer bested Jay Lethal and Roderick Strong; ROH Champion Adam Cole won a three-way over Michael Elgin and Jay Briscoe.
