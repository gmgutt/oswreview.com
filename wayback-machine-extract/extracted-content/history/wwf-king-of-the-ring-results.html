WWF King of the Ring Results

WWF/E KING OF THE RESULTS 

13th June 1993: “WWF King of the Ring 1993”: Dayton, OH – 8,000 sellout (7,000 paid)
($78,000 live gate)
Dark match: Papa Shango beat Owen Hart.
PPV Broadcast: Bret Hart dropped Razor Ramon (KOTR Tournament- Quarter-Final); Curt Hennig downed Mr Hughes by DQ (KOTR Tournament- Quarter-Final); Bam Bam Bigelow bested Jim Duggan (KOTR Tournament- Quarter-Final); Tatanka battled Lex Luger to a draw (KOTR Tournament- Quarter-Final); Bret Hart dropped Curt Hennig (KOTR Tournament – Semi-Final); Yokozuna pinned WWF World Champion Hulk Hogan to win the title; The Smoking Gunns and The Steiners overcame The Headshrinkers and WWF World Tag Champions Ted Dibiase and IRS; WWF Intercontinental Champion Shawn Michaels toppled Crush; Bret Hart rolled over Bam Bam Bigelow (KOTR Tournament – Final).

19th June 1994: “WWF King of the Ring 1994”: Baltimore, MD – 12,000 sellout
($170,000 live gate)
Dark match: Sparky Plugg beat Bob Holly.
PPV Broadcast: Razor Ramon pinned Bam Bam Bigelow (KOTR Quarter-Final); IRS downed Mabel (KOTR Quarter-Final); Owen Hart toppled Tatanka (KOTR Quarter-Final); The 1-2-3 Kid overturned Jeff Jarrett (KOTR Quarter-Final); WWF Intercontinental Champion Diesel rolled over WWF World Champion Bret Hart by DQ; Razor Ramon downed IRS (KOTR Semi-Final); Owen Hart toppled The 1-2-3 Kid (KOTR Semi-Final); WWF World Tag Champions The Headshrinkers overturned Yokozuna and Crush; Owen Hart bested Razor Ramon (KOTR Final); Roddy Piper pinned Jerry Lawler.

25th June 1995: “WWF King of the Ring 1995”: Philadelphia, PA – 16,590 (14,181 paid)
($311,680 live gate)
Sunday Night Slam: Savio Vega beat IRS (KOTR Qualifier).
PPV Broadcast: Savio Vega downed Yokozuna by count-out (KOTR Quarter-Final); The Roadie defeated Bob Holly (KOTR Quarter-Final); Shawn Michaels battled Kama to a draw (KOTR Quarter-Final); Mabel pinned The Undertaker (KOTR Quarter-Final); Savio Vega downed The Roadie (KOTR Semi-Final); Bret Hart toppled Jerry Lawler in a “Kiss my Foot” match; Mabel bested Savio Vega (KOTR Final); WWF World Champion Diesel and Bam Bam Bigelow rolled over Sid Vicious and Tatanka.

23rd June 1996: “WWF King of the Ring 1996”: Milwaukee, WI – 8,762 sellout
($142,568 live gate)
Dark matches: Hunter Hearst Helmsley defeated Aldo Montoya.
Free for All: Skip and Zip beat Marty Jannetty and Leif Cassidy.
PPV Broadcast: Steve Austin downed Marc Mero (KOTR 1996 – Semi-Final); Jake Roberts dropped Vader by DQ (KOTR 1996 – Semi-Final); WWF World Tag Champions The Smoking Gunns overcame The Godwinns; The Ultimate Warrior bested Jerry Lawler; Mankind toppled The Undertaker; Ahmed Johnson overturned WWF Intercontinental Champion Goldust to win the title; Steve Austin overcame Jake Roberts (KOTR 1996 – Final); WWF World Champion Shawn Michaels rolled over Davey Boy Smith. Curt Hennig was outside guest referee.

8th June 1997: “WWF King of the Ring 1997”: Providence, RI – 9,312
($202,963 live gate)
($74,672 merchandise sales)
Free for All: The Headbangers overcame Bart Gunn and Jesse Jammes.
PPV Broadcast: Hunter Hearst Helmsley bested Ahmed Johnson (KOTR 1997 – Semi-Final); Mankind overturned Jerry Lawler (KOTR 1997 – Semi-Final); Goldust pinned Crush; WWF Intercontinental Champion Owen Hart, WWF European Champion Davey Boy Smith, and Jim Neidhart defeated Sid Vicious and The Legion of Doom; Hunter Hearst Helmsley bested Mankind (KOTR 1997 – Final); Steve Austin battled Shawn Michaels to a double-DQ; WWF World Champion The Undertaker rolled over Faarooq.

28th June 1998: “WWF King of the Ring 1998”: Pittsburgh, PA – 17,087 sellout (15,505 paid)
($539,660 live gate)
($148,569 mechandise sales)
The Headbangers and WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku defeated Mens Teioh, Sho Funaki and Dick Togo; Ken Shamrock toppled Jeff Jarrett (KOTR 1998 – Semi Final);  WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock downed Dan Severn (KOTR 1998 – Semi Final); Brian Christopher and Scott Taylor dropped Al Snow (and Head) in a handicap match. Jerry Lawler was guest referee. X-Pac pinned Owen Hart; WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg overcame NWA Tag Team Champions Bob Holly and Bart Gunn; Ken Shamrock overturned WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock in a non-title match (KOTR 1998 – Semi-Final); The Undertaker bested Mankind in a “Hell-in-a-Cell” match; Kane rolled over WWF World Champion Steve Austin in a “first blood” match to win the title.

27th June 1999: “WWF King of the Ring 1999”: Greensboro, NC – 19,761 sellout (18,574 paid)
($574,200 live gate)
($110,692 merchandise sales)
X-Pac beat Hardcore Holly by DQ (KOTR 1999 – Quarter-Final); Kane downed The Big Show (KOTR 1999 – Quarter-Final); Billy Gunn bested Ken Shamrock by stoppage (KOTR 1999 – Quarter-Final); Road Dogg pinned Chyna (KOTR 1999 – Quarter-Final); Matt and Jeff Hardy dropped Edge and Christian; Billy Gunn toppled Kane (KOTR 1999 – Semi-Final); X-Pac downed Road Dogg (KOTR 1999 – Semi-Final); WWF World Champion The Undertaker dropped The Rock; Billy Gunn toppled X-Pac (KOTR 1999 – Final); Vince McMahon and Shane McMahon rolled over Steve Austin in a handicap “ 100% Control of the Company” ladder match.

25th June 2000: “WWF King of the Ring 2000”: Boston, MA – 17,651 sellout (14,464 paid)
($865,778 live gate)
PPV Broadcast: WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi toppled Chris Benoit by DQ (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Val Venis overturned WWF European Champion Eddie Guerrero in a non-title match (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Crash Holly overcame Bull Buchanon (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Kurt Angle toppled Chris Jericho (KOTR 2000 – Quarter-Final); Edge and Christian downed WWF World Tag Champions Scotty 2 Hotty and Brian Christopher, The Hardy Boys, and Test and Albert in a four-way elimination match to win the titles; WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi toppled Val Venis (KOTR 2000 – Semi-Final); Kurt Angle toppled Crash Holly (KOTR 2000 – Semi-Final); WWF Hardcore Champion Pat Patterson battled Gerald Brisco to a no-contest in an “Evening Gown” match (Crash Holly overcame WWF Hardcore Champion Pat Patterson to win the title); X-Pac and Road Dogg defeated The Dudley Boys in a handicap “dumpster” match; Kurt Angle toppled WWF Intercontinental Champion Rikishi in a non-title match (KOTR 2000 – Final); The Rock, The Undertaker, and Kane toppled WWF World Champion Triple H, Vince and Shane McMahon. By virtue of the win, Rock was awarded the WWF World Title.

24th June 2001: “WWF King of the Ring 2001”: East Rutherford, NJ – 16,024 sellout
($1,132,375 live gate)
($131,673 merchandise sales)
Heat: (06/24): WWF European Champion Matt Hardy beat Justin Credible.
PPV Broadcast: Kurt Angle toppled Christian (KOTR 2001 – Semi-Final); Edge bested Rhyno (KOTR 2001 – Semi-Final); WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys overturned Spike Dudley and WWF Intercontinental Champion Kane; Edge dropped Kurt Angle (KOTR 2001 – Final); WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Jeff Hardy beat X-Pac; Kurt Angle toppled Shane McMahon in a streetfight; WWF World Champion Steve Austin bested Chris Jericho and Chris Benoit in a three-way.

23rd June 2002: “WWE King of the Ring 2002”: Columbus, OH – 14,200
($897,545 live gate)
Dark match: The Hardy Boys beat Raven and Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Intercontinental Champion Rob Van Dam rolled over Chris Jericho (KOTR 2002 – Semi-Final); Brock Lesnar beat Test (KOTR 2002 – Semi-Final); Jamie Noble beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion The Hurricane to win the title; Ric Flair overturned Eddie Guerrero; Molly Holly downed WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled Hulk Hogan; Brock Lesnar beat WWE Intercontinental Champion Rob Van Dam in a non-title match (KOTR 2002 – Final); WWE World Champion The Undertaker bested Triple H.
