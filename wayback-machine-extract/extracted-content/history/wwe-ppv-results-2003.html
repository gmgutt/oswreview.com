WWE PPV Results 2003

WWE PPV RESULTS (2003)

See also: WWE PPV Statistics 2003

19th January 2003: “WWE Royal Rumble 2003”: Boston, MA – 14,712
($850,000 live gate)
Heat: (01/19): Spike Dudley beat Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: Brock Lesnar dropped The Big Show; The Dudley Boys overturned WWE World Tag Champions Lance Storm and William Regal to win the titles; Torrie Wilson bested Dawn Marie; Scott Steiner toppled World Heavyweight Champion Triple H by DQ; WWE World Champion Kurt Angle rolled over Chris Benoit; Brock Lesnar won the Royal Rumble match by last eliminating The Undertaker.

23rd February 2003: “WWE No Way Out 2003”: Montreal, QUE – 15,100
($595,089 live gate)
Heat: (02/23): Rey Mysterio dropped Jamie Noble.
PPV Broadcast: Chris Jericho bested Jeff Hardy; WWE World Tag Champions William Regal and Lance Storm defeated Rob Van Dam and Kane; Matt Hardy beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Billy Kidman to win the title; The Undertaker bested The Big Show; Brock Lesnar and Chris Benoit toppled WWE World Champion Kurt Angle and WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas in a handicap match; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Scott Steiner; Steve Austin bested Eric Bischoff; The Rock pinned Hulk Hogan.

30th March 2003: “WWE Wrestlemania XIX”: Seattle, WA – 54,097 sellout (52,000 paid)
($2,760,635 live gate)
Heat: (03/30): WWE World Tag Champions Lance Storm and Chief Morley downed Rob Van Dam and Kane.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Cruiserweight Champion Matt Hardy pinned Rey Mysterio; The Undertaker bested The Big Show and Albert in a handicap match; Trish Stratus overcame WWE Women’s Champion Victoria and Jazz in a three-way to win the title; WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas downed Eddie and Chavo Guerrero Jr, and Chris Benoit and Rhyno in a three-way; Shawn Michaels overturned Chris Jericho; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Booker T; Hulk Hogan bested Vince McMahon in a streetfight; The Rock pinned Steve Austin; Brock Lesnar dropped WWE World Champion Kurt Angle to win the title.

27th April 2003: “WWE Backlash 2003”: Worcester, MA – 10,000
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (04/27): Scott Steiner dropped Rico.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Team Champions Charlie Haas and Shelton Benjamin bested Eddie and Chavo Guerrero Jr; Sean O’Haire beat Rikishi; WWE World Tag Champions Rob Van Dam and Kane toppled The Dudley Boys. Chief Morley was guest referee; Jazz overturned WWE Women’s Champion Trish Stratus to win the title; The Big Show toppled Rey Mysterio; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped John Cena; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H, Ric Flair, and Chris Jericho bested Kevin Nash, Shawn Michaels, and Booker T; Bill Goldberg pinned The Rock.

18th May 2003: “WWE Judgment Day 2003”: Charlotte, NC – 13,000
($650,000 live gate)
Heat: (05/18): The Hurricane dropped Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: John Cena, Chuck Palumbo, and Johnny Stamboli beat Chris Benoit, Rhyno, and Brian Kendrick; Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree downed Scott Steiner and Test; Eddie Guerrero and Tajiri dropped WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas in a ladder match to win the titles; Christian won a battle royal to win the vacant WWE Intercontinental Title; Torrie Wilson bested Sable in a bikini challenge; Mr America (a masked Hulk Hogan) pinned Roddy Piper; Kevin Nash defeated World Heavyweight Champion Triple H by DQ; WWE Women’s Champion Jazz beat Trish Stratus, Victoria, and Jacquelyn in a four-way; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped The Big Show in a stretcher match.

15th June 2003: “WWE Bad Blood 2003”: Houston, TX – 10,000
($500,000 live gate)
Heat: (06/15): Ivory beat Molly Holly.
PPV Broadcast: Christopher Nowinski and Rodney Mack beat The Dudley Boys; Scott Steiner dropped Test; Booker T overturned WWE Intercontinental Champion Christian by DQ; Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree downed WWE World Tag Champions Rob Van Dam and Kane to win the titles; Bill Goldberg pinned Chris Jericho; Ric Flair overturned Shawn Michaels; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Kevin Nash in a “Hell in a Cell” match. Mick Foley was guest referee.

27th July 2003: “WWE Vengeance 2003”: Denver, CO – 9,500
($475,000 live gate)
Heat: (07/27): Ultimo Dragon bested Kanyon.
PPV Broadcast: Eddie Guerrero overcame Chris Benoit  to win the vacant WWE US Title (WWE US Title Tournament – Final;); Jamie Noble toppled Billy Gunn; Bradshaw won the “APA Invitational Bar Room Brawl”; WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas downed WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio and Billy Kidman; Sable toppled Stephanie McMahon in a no count-out encounter; The Undertaker bested John Cena; Vince McMahon toppled Zach Gowen; Kurt Angle toppled WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar and The Big Show in a three-way to win the title.

24th August 2003: “WWE Summerslam 2003”: Phoenix, AZ – 16,113 sellout
($715,000 live gate)
Heat: (08/24):  Matt Hardy beat Zach Gowen by forfeit; WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio dropped Shannon Moore.
PPV Broadcast: WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree downed The Dudley Boys; The Undertaker bested Albert; Shane McMahon toppled Eric Bischoff in a no-DQ, “falls count anywhere” match; WWE US Champion Eddie Guerrero overcame Chris Benoit, Tajiri, and Rhyno in a four-way; WWE World Champion Kurt Angle toppled Brock Lesnar; Kane toppled Rob Van Dam in a “no holds barred” match; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Shawn Michaels, Bill Goldberg, Kevin Nash, Randy Orton, and Chris Jericho in an “Elimination Chamber” match.

21st September 2003: “WWE Unforgiven 2003”: Hershey, PA – 10,347 
($560,000 live gate)
Heat: (09/21): Maven bested Steven Richards.
PPV Broadcast: The Dudley Boys overturned Rob Conway, and WWE World Tag Champions Sylvain Grenier and Rene Dupree in a handicap tables match to win the titles; Test beat Scott Steiner in a “Stacy’s services vs. Steiner’s services” encounter; Randy Orton bested Shawn Michaels; Lita and Trish Stratus overcame WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly and Gail Kim; Kane toppled Shane McMahon in a “last man standing” match; WWE Intercontinental Champion Christian downed Rob Van Dam and Chris Jericho in a three-way; Al Snow and Jonathan Coachman bested Jerry Lawler and Jim Ross in a “Winner announces Raw” match; Bill Goldberg pinned World Heavyweight Champion Triple H to win the title in a “Title vs. Career” encounter.

19th October 2003: “WWE No Mercy 2003”: Baltimore, MD – 8,500
($500,000 live gate)
Heat: (10/19): Billy Kidman bested Shannon Moore.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Cruiserweight Champion Tajiri beat Rey Mysterio; Chris Benoit toppled Albert; Zach Gowen bested Matt Hardy; Doug and Danny Basham beat The Acolytes; Vince McMahon dropped Stephanie McMahon in an “I Quit” match; Kurt Angle toppled John Cena; The Big Show toppled WWE US Champion Eddie Guerrero to win the title; WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar dropped The Undertaker in a “Biker Chain” match.

16th November 2003: “WWE Survivor Series 2003”: Dallas, TX – 13,487
($723,455 live gate)
Heat: (11/16): WWE Cruiserweight Champion Tajiri beat Jamie Noble .
PPV Broadcast: Kurt Angle, Chris Benoit, John Cena, Hardcore Holly, and Bradshaw defeated WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar, WWE US Champion The Big Show, Matt Morgan, Nathan Jones, and Albert in a “Survivor Series” elimination match; WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly downed Lita; Kane bested Shane McMahon in an “ambulance” match; WWE Tag Team Champions Doug and Danny Basham dropped Eddie and Chavo Guerrero Jr; Chris Jericho, Christian, Scott Steiner, Randy Orton, and Mark Henry rolled over Booker T, WWE Intercontinental Champion Rob Van Dam, Shawn Michaels, and WWE World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys in a “Survivor Series” elimination match; Vince McMahon bested The Undertaker in a “Buried Alive” match; World Heavyweight Champion Bill Goldberg pinned Triple H.

14th December 2003: “WWE Armageddon 2003”: Orlando, FL – 9,000
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (12/14): Rico beat Jon Heidenreich.
PPV Broadcast: Booker T overturned Mark Henry; Randy Orton bested WWE Intercontinental Champion Rob Van Dam to win the title. Mick Foley was guest referee; Chris Jericho and Christian downed Trish Stratus and Lita; Shawn Michaels overturned Batista; Ric Flair and Batista overturned WWE World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys, Scott Steiner and Test, Rob Conway and Rene Dupree, The Hurricane and Rosey, Mark Jindrak and Garrison Cade, and Lance Storm and Val Venis to win the titles in a “tag team turmoil” match; WWE Women’s Champion Molly Holly downed Ivory; Triple H rolled over Kane and World Heavyweight Champion Bill Goldberg in a three-way to win the title.
