WWE No Way Out Results

WWE NO WAY OUT RESULTS

15th February 1998:  “WWF IYH: No Way Out”:  Houston, TX- 16,110 sellout (14,280 paid)
($241,992 live gate)
($166,343 merchandise sales)
The Headbangers defeated Marc Mero and TAFKA Goldust; WWF Light Heavyweight Champion Taka Michinoku pinned Pantera; The Godwinns downed The Quebecers; Bradshaw dropped NWA North American Champion Jeff Jarrett by DQ; Ken Shamrock, Ahmed Johnson, Skull, Eight-Ball and Chainz overcame Faarooq, WWF Intercontinental Champion The Rock, D-Lo Brown, Kama and Mark Henry in a “war-of-attrition” match; Kane bested Vader; Steve Austin, WWF European Champion Owen Hart, Cactus Jack and Terry Funk rolled over Triple H, Savio Vega and WWF World Tag Champions Road Dogg and Billy Gunn.

27th February 2000: “WWF No Way Out 2000”: Hartford, CT – 12,551 sellout
($451,625 live gate)
($97,126 merchandise sales)
WWF European Champion Kurt Angle pinned WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho to win the title; The Dudley Boys overcame WWF World Tag Champions Billy Gunn and Road Dogg to win the titles; Mark Henry beat Viscera; Edge and Christian downed Matt and Jeff Hardy; Tazz toppled The Big Bossman by DQ; X-Pac bested Kane in a no-holds-barred match; Rikishi and Too Cool overturned Chris Benoit, Dean Malenko and Perry Saturn; The Big Show toppled The Rock; WWF World Champion Triple H  rolled over Cactus Jack in a “Title vs. Career” Hell-in-a-Cell match.

25th February 2001: “WWF No Way Out 2001”: Las Vegas, NV – 15,223 sellout (13,119 paid)
($683,955 live gate)
($129,478 merchandise sales)
Heat: (02/25): Rikishi dropped Matt Hardy by DQ.
PPV Broadcast: The Big Show beat WWF Hardcore Champion Raven to win the title; WWF Intercontinental Champion Chris Jericho defeated Chris Benoit, Eddie Guerrero, and X-Pac in a four-way; Stephanie McMahon pinned Trish Stratus; Triple H toppled Steve Austin in a “Three Stages of Hell” encounter; Steven Richards downed Jerry Lawler; WWF World Tag Champions The Dudley Boys bested Edge and Christian, and The Undertaker and Kane in a three-way tables match; The Rock rolled over WWF World Champion Kurt Angle to win the title.

17th February 2002: “WWF No Way Out 2002”: Milwaukee, WI – 15,235 sellout (13,484 paid)
($966,150 live gate)
($139,591 merchandise sales)
Heat: (02/17): WWF European Champion Diamond Dallas Page toppled The Big Bossman.
PPV Broadcast: The Acolytes overturned Scotty 2 Hotty and Albert, Lance Storm and Christian, Matt and Jeff Hardy, The Dudley Boys, and Billy Gunn and Chuck Palumbo in a “tag team turmoil” match; Rob Van Dam rolled over Goldust; WWF World Tag Champions Tazz and Spike Dudley beat Test and Booker T; WWF Intercontinental Champion William Regal downed Edge in a “brass-knuckles-on-a-pole” match; The Rock pinned The Undertaker; Kurt Angle toppled Triple H; WWF World Champion Chris Jericho bested Steve Austin.

23rd February 2003: “WWE No Way Out 2003”: Montreal, QUE – 15,100
($595,089 live gate)
Heat: (02/23): Rey Mysterio dropped Jamie Noble.
PPV Broadcast: Chris Jericho bested Jeff Hardy; WWE World Tag Champions William Regal and Lance Storm defeated Rob Van Dam and Kane; Matt Hardy beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Billy Kidman to win the title; The Undertaker bested The Big Show; Brock Lesnar and Chris Benoit toppled WWE World Champion Kurt Angle and WWE Tag Team Champions Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas in a handicap match; World Heavyweight Champion Triple H rolled over Scott Steiner; Steve Austin bested Eric Bischoff; The Rock pinned Hulk Hogan.

15th February 2004: “WWE No Way Out 2004”: San Francisco, CA – 11,000 sellout (9,000 paid)
($450,000 live gate)
Heat: (02/15): Tajiri, Akio, and Sakoda defeated Ultimo Dragon, Billy Kidman, and Paul London.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Tag Team Champions Scotty 2 Hotty and Rikishi defeated Shaniqua, Doug and Danny Basham in a handicap match; Jamie Noble toppled Nidia; Shelton Benjamin and Charlie Haas downed The Acolytes; Hardcore Holly dropped Rhyno; Chavo Guerrero Jr beat WWE Cruiserweight Champion Rey Mysterio to win the title; Kurt Angle toppled WWE US Champion The Big Show and John Cena in a three-way; Eddie Guerrero overcame WWE World Champion Brock Lesnar to win the title.

20th February 2005: “WWE No Way Out 2005”: Pittsburgh, PA – 9,500 (8,000 paid)
($492,000 live gate)
Heat: (02/20): Hardcore Holly and Charlie Haas beat Kenzo Suzuki and Rene Dupree.
PPV Broadcast: Eddie Guerrero and Rey Mysterio downed WWE Tag Champions Doug Basham and Danny Basham to win the titles; Booker T dropped John Heidenreich by DQ; Chavo Guerrero Jr won a gauntlet match over WWE Cruiserweight Champion Sho Funaki, Spike Dudley, Paul London, Shannon Moore, and Akio to win the title; The Undertaker toppled Luther Reigns; WWE US Champion John Cena bested Kurt Angle (WWE World Title #1 Contender Tournament – Final); WWE World Champion John Bradshaw Layfield rolled over The Big Show in a “barbed wire cage” match.

19th February 2006: “WWE No Way Out 2006”: Baltimore, MD – 11,000 sellout
($575,000 live gate)
Heat: (02/19): The Boogeyman defeated Simon Dean.
PPV Broadcast: WWE Cruiserweight Champion Gregory Helms overcame Scotty 2 Hotty, Psychosis, Nunzio, Super Crazy, Brian Kendrick, Paul London and Kid Kash in a nine-way match;  John Bradshaw Layfield dropped Bobby Lashley; Tatanka and Matt Hardy overturned WWE Tag Champions Joey Mercury and Johnny Nitro in a non-title match; Chris Benoit toppled WWE US Champion Booker T to win the title; Randy Orton bested Rey Mysterio; World Heavyweight Champion Kurt Angle rolled over The Undertaker.

18th February 2007: “WWE No Way Out 2007”: Los Angeles, CA – 14,000 sellout
($750,000 live gate)
Dark match: Rob Van Dam downed Shelton Benjamin.
PPV Broadcast: WWE US Champion Chris Benoit, WWE Intercontinental Champion Jeff Hardy, and Matt Hardy downed MVP, Johnny Nitro and Joey Mercury; Chavo Guerrero Jr won a 8-man gauntlet match to win the WWE Cruiserweight title: Scotty 2 Hotty pinned Daivari, Gregory Helms downed Scotty 2 Hotty, Gregory Helms pinned Sho Funaki, Gregory Helms pinned Shannon Moore, Jimmy Wang Yang pinned Gregory Helms,  Jimmy Wang Yang pinned Jamie Noble, Chavo Guerrero Jr pinned Jimmy Wang Yang; Finlay and Hornswoggle beat The Boogeyman and Little Boogeyman; Kane bested King Booker; WWE Tag Champions Paul London and Brian Kendrick overcame Deuce and Domino; Mr Kennedy dropped ECW Champion Bobby Lashley by DQ; WWE World Tag Champions John Cena and Shawn Michaels toppled World Heavyweight Champion Batista and The Undertaker.

17th February 2008: “WWE No Way Out 2008”: Las Vegas, NV – 13,306 sellout
($772,720 live gate)
Dark match: Kane beat Shelton Benjamin.
PPV Broadcast: ECW Champion Chavo Guerrero Jr pinned CM Punk; The Undertaker won an “Elimination Chamber” match over The Great Khali, Batista, WWE US Champion MVP, Finlay, and Big Daddy V; Ric Flair downed Mr Kennedy; World Heavyweight Champion Edge bested Rey Mysterio; John Cena toppled WWE World Champion Randy Orton by DQ; Triple H won an “Elimination Chamber” match over WWE Intercontinental Champion Jeff Hardy, Shawn Michaels, John Bradshaw Layfield, Chris Jericho, and Umaga.

15th February 2009: “WWE No Way Out 2009”: Seattle, WA – 11,362
($539,505 live gate)
Dark match: WWE Women’s Champion Melina beat Beth Phoenix.
PPV Broadcast: Triple H overcame WWE World Champion Edge, Jeff Hardy, The Undertaker, The Big Show and Vladimir Kozlov in an “Elimination Chamber” match to win the title; Randy Orton toppled Shane McMahon in a “no-holds-barred” encounter; ECW Champion Jack Swagger dropped Finlay; Shawn Michaels rolled over John Bradshaw Layfield; Edge bested World Heavyweight Champion John Cena, Rey Mysterio, Kane, Mike Knox and Chris Jericho in an “Elimination Chamber” match to win the title.
